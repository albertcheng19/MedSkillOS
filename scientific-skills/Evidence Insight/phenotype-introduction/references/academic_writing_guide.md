# Academic Writing Guide for Biomedical Phenotypes

## Structure Requirements

### 1. Concept Section (≥800 words)
**Must include:**
- Precise phenotype definition with English terminology in parentheses
- Detailed biological characteristics and features
- Cellular-level functions and physiological roles
- Historical development timeline and key discoveries
- Background knowledge and context

### 2. Mechanism Section (≥800 words)  
**Must include:**
- Step-by-step occurrence process
- Molecular mechanisms and pathways
- Cellular impacts and consequences
- Key elements and critical components
- Precise academic terminology for each concept

### 3. Regulation Section (≥800 words)
**Must include:**
- Regulatory principles and control mechanisms
- All participating molecules and signaling pathways
- Feedback loops and modulation systems
- Phenotype crosstalk with other cellular processes
- Nested relationships and interactions

### 4. Marker Detection (≥500 words, ≥5 markers)
**Must include:**
- Minimum 5 key marker molecules
- Detection principle for each marker
- Specific detection methods and techniques
- Technical details and experimental considerations

## Language Style Guidelines

### Required Style
- **Language**: Simplified Chinese
- **Tone**: Formal academic
- **Terminology**: Precise biomedical terminology
- **Structure**: Clear hierarchical organization
- **Accuracy**: Scientifically accurate descriptions

### Prohibited Elements
- ❌ Casual or colloquial language
- ❌ Overly simplified explanations  
- ❌ Personal opinions or subjective statements
- ❌ Unsupported claims or speculation
- ❌ Incomplete or vague descriptions

### Quality Standards
- **Word count**: Strict adherence to minimum requirements
- **Technical depth**: Appropriate level of detail for academic audience
- **Completeness**: Comprehensive coverage of all required aspects
- **Accuracy**: Scientifically valid information
- **Clarity**: Clear and unambiguous explanations

## Common Biomedical Phenotypes

### Cell Death Phenotypes
- **Pyroptosis**: Caspase-1 mediated inflammatory cell death- **Apoptosis**: Programmed cell death- **Necroptosis**: Programmed necrosis- **Ferroptosis**: Iron-dependent cell death- **Autophagy**: Cellular self-digestion
### Cellular Stress Responses  
- **Mitophagy**: Mitochondrial quality control- **Endoplasmic reticulum stress (ER Stress)**: Unfolded protein response- **Oxidative Stress**: Reactive oxygen species response- **Heat Shock Response**: Protein folding stress
### Metabolic Phenotypes
- **Glycolysis**: Glucose metabolism- **Oxidative Phosphorylation**: ATP production- **Lipid Metabolism**: Lipid processing- **Amino Acid Metabolism**: Protein building blocks
