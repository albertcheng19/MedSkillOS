## Clause 4: Quality Management System

### 4.1 General Requirements

#### 4.1.1 QMS Requirements
- Establish, document, implement, and maintain a QMS
- Maintain its effectiveness in accordance with ISO 13485
- Document the QMS processes and their interactions

#### 4.1.2 Process Approach
- Identify the processes needed for the QMS
- Determine the sequence and interaction of these processes
- Determine criteria and methods needed for effective operation and control
- Ensure the availability of resources and information
- Monitor, measure, and analyze processes
- Implement necessary actions to achieve planned results and maintain effectiveness

#### 4.1.3 Outsourced Processes
- Implement control over any outsourced QMS processes
- Ensure that the type of control is documented within the QMS
- Outsourcing does not relieve the organization of responsibility

#### 4.1.4 General QMS Requirements
- Establish, document, implement, and maintain QMS requirements according to ISO 13485
- Include medical device requirements and applicable regulatory requirements
- Establish documented procedures for QMS activities

#### 4.1.5 Risk Management
- Establish documented requirements for risk management in product realization
- Maintain risk management records
- Ensure risk management is conducted according to documented requirements

#### 4.1.6 Software Validation
- Validate computer software applications used in the QMS
- Validation must occur before initial use and after changes
- Establish documented methods, including:
  - Risks associated with the software application
  - Validation activities
  - Acceptance criteria
  - User responsibilities
  - Validation records

### 4.2 Documentation Requirements

#### 4.2.1 General
QMS documentation must include:
- Quality policy and quality objectives
- Quality manual
- Documented procedures and records required by ISO 13485
- Documents needed by the organization to ensure effective process operation
- Records required by ISO 13485
- Medical device files as specified by applicable regulatory requirements

#### 4.2.2 Quality Manual
Establish and maintain a quality manual, including:
- Scope of the QMS, including details and justification for any exclusions
- Documented procedures or references to them
- Description of the interaction between QMS processes
- Documentation structure used in the QMS

#### 4.2.3 Medical Device File
Establish and maintain a medical device file for each model or type, including:
- General description, intended use/purpose
- Specifications for labeling and instructions for use
- Product and/or manufacturing specifications
- Specifications for procedures for purchasing, manufacturing, and servicing
- Measurement and monitoring procedures
- Installation requirements (if applicable)
- Risk management documentation
- Verification and validation information
- Design and development documentation (if applicable)

#### 4.2.4 Control of Documents
Establish documented procedures to:
- Approve documents for adequacy prior to issue
- Review, update, and re-approve documents
- Ensure that changes and current revision status of documents are identified
- Ensure that relevant versions are available at points of use
- Ensure that documents remain legible and readily identifiable
- Control the distribution of documents
- Prevent unintended use of obsolete documents
- Apply suitable identification to obsolete documents if retained for any purpose

Document changes must:
- Be reviewed and approved by the original approving function or another designated function
- Have access to relevant background information
- Be identified in the document or appropriate attachments

#### 4.2.5 Control of Records
Establish documented procedures for:
- Identification, storage, security, integrity, retrieval, retention period, and disposition
- Records must remain legible, readily identifiable, and retrievable
- Changes to records must remain identifiable
- Retention period must be at least the lifetime of the medical device
- Records can be stored on any medium but must remain retrievable

## Clause 5: Management Responsibility

### 5.1 Management Commitment
Top management must provide evidence of commitment through:
- Communicating the importance of meeting regulatory and customer requirements
- Establishing the quality policy
- Establishing quality objectives
- Conducting management reviews
- Ensuring the availability of resources

### 5.2 Customer Focus
- Determine customer requirements and regulatory requirements
- Ensure customer requirements are met to enhance satisfaction
- Maintain documented requirements related to medical devices

### 5.3 Quality Policy
- Appropriate to the purpose of the organization
- Includes a commitment to comply with requirements and maintain QMS effectiveness
- Provides a framework for establishing quality objectives
- Communicated and understood within the organization
- Reviewed for continuing suitability

### 5.4 Planning

#### 5.4.1 Quality Objectives
- Establish quality objectives at relevant functions and levels
- Must be measurable and consistent with the quality policy
- Objectives must support conformity to product requirements

#### 5.4.2 QMS Planning
- Plan to meet general requirements and quality objectives
- Maintain QMS integrity when changes are planned and implemented
- Document the planning process

### 5.5 Responsibility, Authority and Communication

#### 5.5.1 Responsibility and Authority
- Define and communicate responsibilities and authorities
- Document the roles of personnel who manage, perform, and verify QMS work
- Identify the interrelationship of all personnel

#### 5.5.2 Management Representative
Appoint a member of management with responsibility for:
- Ensuring QMS processes are established, implemented, and maintained
- Reporting to top management on QMS performance and any need for improvement
- Ensuring the promotion of awareness of regulatory and customer requirements throughout the organization

#### 5.5.3 Internal Communication
- Ensure communication processes are established
- Ensure communication regarding the effectiveness of the QMS

### 5.6 Management Review

#### 5.6.1 General
- Review the QMS at planned intervals (at least annually)
- Review to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness
- Include assessment of opportunities for improvement
- Maintain management review records

#### 5.6.2 Review Input
Includes:
- Audit results
- Customer feedback
- Process performance and product conformity
- Status of preventive and corrective actions
- Follow-up actions from previous management reviews
- Changes that could affect the QMS
- Recommendations for improvement
- New or revised applicable regulatory requirements

#### 5.6.3 Review Output
Includes decisions and actions related to:
- Improvement of QMS effectiveness and its processes
- Product improvement related to customer requirements
- Resource needs
- Changes needed to maintain QMS effectiveness

## Clause 6: Resource Management

### 6.1 Provision of Resources
Determine and provide resources needed to:
- Implement and maintain the QMS and its effectiveness
- Meet regulatory and customer requirements

### 6.2 Human Resources

#### 6.2 General
Personnel performing work affecting product quality must be competent based on:
- Education, training, skills, and experience
- Documented evidence of competence

#### 6.3 Infrastructure
Determine, provide, and maintain infrastructure, including:
- Buildings, workspace, and associated utilities
- Process equipment (hardware and software)
- Supporting services

Infrastructure maintenance requirements:
- Document requirements including maintenance activities
- Document requirements when maintenance activities can affect product quality
- Maintain records of maintenance activities

### 6.4 Work Environment and Contamination Control

#### 6.4.1 Work Environment
- Determine and manage the work environment needed to achieve product conformity
- Document requirements for the work environment
- Document requirements if the work environment can have an adverse effect on product quality

#### 6.4.2 Contamination Control
- Where applicable, document requirements for control of contaminated or potentially contaminated products
- Establish specific arrangements for controlling contaminated products

## Clause 7: Product Realization

### 7.1 Planning of Product Realization
Plan and develop the processes needed for product realization, including:
- Quality objectives and requirements for the product
- Need to establish processes, documents, and resources
- Required verification, validation, monitoring, measurement, inspection, handling, storage, distribution, and traceability
- Records providing evidence of conformity

Risk management requirements:
- Establish documented requirements for risk management throughout product realization
- Maintain risk management records

### 7.2 Customer-related Processes

#### 7.2.1 Determination of Requirements
Determine:
- Requirements specified by the customer, including delivery and post-delivery activities
- Requirements not stated by the customer but necessary for intended use
- Applicable regulatory requirements
- Any additional requirements determined by the organization

#### 7.2.2 Review of Requirements
- Review product requirements before commitment
- Ensure requirements are defined and documented
- Ensure differences are resolved
- Ensure ability to meet requirements
- Maintain records of review results and follow-up actions

#### 7.2.3 Communication
Establish and document effective arrangements for communicating with customers regarding:
- Product information
- Enquiries, contracts, or order handling and amendments
- Customer feedback, including complaints
- Advisory notices

### 7.3 Design and Development

#### 7.3.1 General
- Establish, document, and maintain design and development procedures
- Prepare design and development plans for each medical device
- Maintain design and development files

#### 7.3.2 Design and Development Planning
Plan and control design and development, including:
- Design and development stages
- Required review, verification, and validation activities
- Responsibilities and authorities
- Resources and interfaces
- Update plans as design progresses
- Document plans

#### 7.3.3 Design and Development Inputs
- Determine inputs related to product requirements
- Include functional, performance, usability, and safety requirements
- Include applicable regulatory requirements and standards
- Include applicable outputs of risk management
- Include applicable information from previous similar designs
- Review inputs for adequacy and completeness
- Resolve incomplete, ambiguous, or conflicting requirements
- Maintain records

#### 7.3.4 Design and Development Outputs
Provide outputs that:
- Meet design input requirements
- Provide appropriate information for purchasing, production, and service
- Contain or reference product acceptance criteria
- Specify characteristics essential for safety and proper use
- Document outputs and maintain as records

#### 7.3.5 Design and Development Review
- Perform systematic reviews at appropriate stages
- Evaluate ability to meet requirements
- Identify problems and propose actions
- Include representatives of relevant functions
- Maintain records, including results and follow-up actions

#### 7.3.6 Design and Development Verification
- Perform verification according to planned arrangements
- Ensure outputs meet input requirements
- Maintain records of verification results and follow-up actions

#### 7.3.7 Design and Development Validation
- Perform validation according to planned arrangements
- Ensure product meets specific application or intended use
- Perform validation prior to delivery or implementation
- Include validation under defined operating conditions
- Maintain records of validation results and follow-up actions

#### 7.3.8 Design and Development Transfer
- Document procedures for transfer of design to manufacturing
- Verify manufacturing outputs meet design outputs
- Ensure materials, production, quality control, and service specifications are appropriate
- Maintain records

#### 7.3.9 Control of Design and Development Changes
- Identify, document, and control changes
- Review, verify, validate, and approve changes before implementation
- Evaluate effect on constituent parts, work-in-progress, and delivered products
- Maintain records of changes, review results, and follow-up actions

#### 7.3.10 Design and Development File
Establish and maintain a design and development file for each model or type, including:
- Design and development plans
- Design inputs
- Design outputs
- Design review, verification, and validation records
- Design change records
- Risk management documentation

### 7.4 Purchasing

#### 7.4.1 Purchasing Process
- Ensure purchased products conform to purchasing information
- Establish documented processes for purchasing activities
- Establish criteria for supplier evaluation and selection
- Criteria based on ability to provide products according to organization requirements
- Monitor supplier performance
- Maintain records of evaluation and follow-up actions
- Establish processes for notifying suppliers of changes in purchasing requirements

#### 7.4.2 Purchasing Information
Purchasing information must include:
- Approval requirements for product, procedures, processes, and equipment
- Personnel qualification requirements
- Quality management system requirements
- Requirements for notifying the organization of nonconforming products
- Agreement for suppliers to notify of changes in purchased products
- Agreement for purchasing information to be communicated to sub-tier suppliers

#### 7.4.3 Verification of Purchased Product
- Establish and implement inspection or other activities to ensure conformity
- Record the extent of verification
- When customer intends to verify at supplier premises, verification must be performed at the supplier
- Record verification arrangements and method of product release

### 7.5 Production and Service Provision

#### 7.5.1 Control of Production and Service Provision
Plan and perform production under controlled conditions, including:
- Availability of documented procedures and work instructions
- Availability of suitable infrastructure and work environment
- Availability of monitoring and measuring equipment
- Availability and use of suitable monitoring and measurement activities
- Implementation of release, delivery, and post-delivery activities
- Implementation of defined labeling and packaging operations
- Implementation of service procedures, as applicable

Document requirements for:
- Control of product cleanliness, where applicable
- Control during installation and verification, where applicable

#### 7.5.2 Cleanliness of Product
Document requirements in the following cases:
- Product is cleaned by the organization prior to sterilization and/or use
- Product cannot be cleaned prior to sterilization
- Product is supplied non-sterile and is to be cleaned prior to sterilization

Establish hygiene requirements for product during manufacturing, handling, and storage.

#### 7.5.3 Installation Activities
If applicable:
- Document installation and verification requirements
- Maintain installation and verification records

#### 7.5.4 Servicing Activities
If servicing is a specified requirement:
- Establish documented service procedures, reference materials, and measurement methods
- Analyze service records for feedback to the post-production phase
- Maintain records of service activities

#### 7.5.5 Particular Requirements for Sterile Medical Devices
Maintain records of sterilization process parameters for each batch.

#### 7.5.6 Validation of Processes
Validate processes where the output cannot be verified by subsequent monitoring or measurement, including:
- Defined criteria for review and approval
- Approval of equipment and qualification of personnel
- Use of specific methods, procedures, and acceptance criteria
- Requirements for records
- Revalidation and its criteria
- Approval of process changes

Document requirements for validation of:
- Computer software used in production and service provision
- Sterilization processes
- Sterile processing
- Cleanroom requirements, where applicable

#### 7.5.7 Particular Requirements for Validation of Processes for Sterilization and Sterile Barrier Systems
Maintain validation records for:
- Sterilization process for each batch
- Sterile barrier systems

#### 7.5.8 Identification
- Establish documented procedures for product identification throughout product realization
- Identify product by suitable means
- Maintain identification records where traceability is required

#### 7.5.9 Traceability

##### 7.5.9.1 General
Establish documented procedures defining the scope of traceability, including:
- Distribution and location of medical devices

##### 7.5.9.2 Particular Requirements
Document procedures to maintain records of:
- Name and address of the shipping package consignee
- Identification of the quantity shipped
- Include requirements of applicable regulations
- Maintain traceability records for a defined period

#### 7.5.10 Customer Property
- Exercise care with customer property while under organization control
- Identify, verify, protect, and safeguard customer property
- If lost, damaged, or unsuitable, record and report to the customer
- Maintain records

#### 7.5.11 Preservation of Product
- Preserve product during internal processing and delivery
- Includes identification, handling, packaging, storage, and protection
- Applies to constituent parts of the product
- Document special handling requirements, where applicable

### 7.6 Control of Monitoring and Measuring Equipment
- Determine monitoring and measurement to be undertaken
- Determine monitoring and measuring equipment needed
- Establish documented procedures for:
  - Calibration or verification at specified intervals prior to use
  - Adjustment or re-adjustment as necessary
  - Identification to determine calibration status
  - Safeguarding from adjustments that would invalidate calibration
  - Protection from damage and deterioration
- Assess and record the validity of previous measurement results when equipment is found not to conform
- Maintain calibration and verification records
- Validate the ability of computer software to satisfy intended use prior to use
- Validate before initial use and re-validate as necessary

## Clause 8: Measurement, Analysis and Improvement

### 8.1 General
- Plan and implement monitoring, measurement, analysis, and improvement processes
- Demonstrate product conformity
- Ensure QMS conformity
- Maintain QMS effectiveness
- Include determination of applicable methods (including statistical techniques)

### 8.2 Monitoring and Measurement

#### 8.2.1 Feedback
Establish documented procedures for feedback, including early warning systems for:
- Post-production information, including complaints
- Requirements for reporting to regulatory authorities
- Potential input into risk management for monitoring and maintaining product requirements
- Potential input into corrective and preventive actions

#### 8.2.2 Complaint Handling
Establish documented procedures for timely complaint handling, including:
- Responsibilities and requirements for receiving, recording, and evaluating complaints
- Responsibilities and requirements for handling, investigating, and evaluating complaints
- Responsibilities and requirements for reporting complaint information to regulatory authorities
- Requirements for notifying the customer of actions taken by the organization
- Requirements to ensure complaint information not handled by the organization is transferred to the organization
- Maintain records of complaints and investigations

#### 8.2.3 Reporting to Regulatory Authorities
Establish documented procedures to:
- Issue notices to regulatory authorities according to applicable requirements
- Issue advisory notices according to applicable requirements
- Maintain records of reporting

#### 8.2.4 Internal Audit
- Conduct internal audits at planned intervals
- Determine if the QMS conforms to ISO 13485 and organization requirements
- Determine if the QMS is effectively implemented and maintained
- Plan audit program considering process importance, changes, and previous audit results
- Define audit criteria, scope, frequency, and methods
- Establish documented procedures for auditing, including responsibilities, requirements, and reporting
- Select objective and impartial auditors
- Maintain records of audits and results
- Identify need for correction or corrective actions
- Perform follow-up activities to verify implementation and effectiveness

#### 8.2.5 Monitoring and Measurement of Processes
- Apply suitable methods for monitoring and measurement of QMS processes
- Demonstrate ability to achieve planned results
- Take correction and corrective actions when planned results are not reached
- Maintain records

#### 8.2.6 Monitoring and Measurement of Product
- Monitor and measure product characteristics to verify conformity
- Perform at appropriate stages according to planned arrangements
- Maintain records showing conformity with acceptance criteria
- Record the authorized person responsible for release
- Ensure product release and delivery do not proceed until planned arrangements are completed
- Allow release by relevant authorized person and customer approval, where applicable

### 8.3 Control of Nonconforming Product

#### 8.3.1 General
- Ensure nonconforming product is identified and controlled
- Establish documented procedures for:
  - Identification, documentation, evaluation, segregation, and disposition
  - Notification of external parties
  - Review of nonconforming product
- Maintain records of nonconformities and subsequent actions (including concessions)

#### 8.3.2 Actions in Response to Nonconforming Product Detected Before Delivery
Handle nonconforming product by:
- Taking action to eliminate the detected nonconformity
- Authorizing its use, release, or acceptance under concession according to applicable regulatory requirements
- Taking action to preclude its original intended use or application
- Taking action appropriate to the effects of the nonconformity when detected after delivery or use

Maintain records of concessions and identify the person authorizing the concession.

#### 8.3.3 Actions in Response to Nonconforming Product Detected After Delivery
When nonconforming product is detected after delivery or use:
- Take action appropriate to the effects of the nonconformity
- Establish documented procedures including requirements for reporting to regulatory authorities
- Maintain records

#### 8.3.4 Rework
Establish documented procedures for rework, including:
- Requirements for evaluating potential adverse effects on the medical device
- Approval prior to implementation
- Records of results and actions, including nonconformity and rework status
- Re-verification after rework
- Document rework procedures before rework begins

### 8.4 Analysis of Data
- Determine, collect, and analyze appropriate data from monitoring and measurement
- Evaluate where continual improvement of QMS effectiveness can be made
- Establish documented procedures for:
  - Analyzing data to provide information on customer satisfaction
  - Analyzing conformity to product requirements
  - Analyzing characteristics and trends of processes and products, including opportunities for preventive action
  - Supplier analysis
  - Analyzing other relevant data, including feedback and outputs of risk management
- Include use of statistical techniques where necessary
- Maintain records of analysis results

### 8.5 Improvement

#### 8.5.1 General
- Identify and implement changes to ensure and maintain QMS effectiveness
- Include use of quality policy, objectives, audit results, data analysis, corrective and preventive actions, and management review

#### 8.5.2 Corrective Action
- Establish documented procedures to:
  - Review nonconformities (including complaints)
  - Determine causes of nonconformities
  - Evaluate the need for action to ensure nonconformities do not recur
  - Plan and document needed action and implement
  - Record results of action taken
  - Review the effectiveness of corrective action taken
- Maintain records, including investigation results and follow-up

#### 8.5.3 Preventive Action
- Establish documented procedures to:
  - Determine potential nonconformities and their causes
  - Evaluate the need for action to prevent occurrence
  - Plan and document needed action and implement
  - Record results of action taken
  - Review the effectiveness of preventive action taken
- Use appropriate sources of information, including:
  - Work processes and operations affecting product quality
  - Concessions
  - Data analysis and risk management outputs
  - Medical device performance data
  - Nonconformity records
- Maintain records, including investigation results and follow-up

## Key Regulatory Updates

### FDA QMSR Harmonization (Effective Feb 2, 2026)
- FDA 21 CFR Part 820 has been harmonized with ISO 13485:2016
- Renamed to QMSR (Quality Management System Regulation)
- Medical Device File (MDF) replaces separate DHF, DMR, and DHR
- Organizations should prepare for transition to a unified documentation approach
