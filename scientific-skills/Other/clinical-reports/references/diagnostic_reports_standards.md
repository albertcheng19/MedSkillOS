# Diagnostic Reporting Standards

## Radiology Reporting Standards

### American College of Radiology (ACR) Guidelines

The ACR provides comprehensive practice parameters for diagnostic imaging reports to ensure quality, consistency, and effective communication.

#### Core Components of a Radiology Report

**1. Patient Demographics**
- Patient name and/or unique identifier
- Date of birth or age
- Gender
- Medical record number (MRN)
- Date and time of the examination
- Referring physician

**2. Procedure/Examination**
- Specific examination performed
- Anatomical site
- Laterality (right, left, bilateral)
- Technique and protocol
- Example: "MRI Brain without and with Contrast"

**3. Clinical Indication**
- Reason for the examination
- Relevant clinical history
- Specific clinical questions
- ICD-10 codes (as required)
- Example: "Headache and visual disturbances. Rule out intracranial mass."

**4. Comparison**
- Relevant prior imaging studies
- Dates of prior studies
- Modalities of prior studies
- Availability of comparisons
- Example: "Comparison: CT head without contrast from 6 months prior (January 15, 2023)"

**5. Technique**
- Imaging parameters and protocols
- Contrast administration details:
  - Type (iodinated, gadolinium-based)
  - Route (IV, oral, rectal)
  - Administered dose
  - Timing of imaging
- Technical quality statement
- Radiation dose (for CT)
- Limitations or technical issues
- Example:
  ```
  Technique: Multiplanar T1 and T2-weighted sequences were obtained through
  the brain without and with IV contrast. 15 mL of gadolinium-based contrast
  agent was administered intravenously. Technical quality is adequate.
  ```

**6. Findings**
- Systematic description of imaging findings
- Organized by anatomical region or organ system
- Measurements of abnormalities (size, volume)
- Specific descriptive terminology
- Relevant positive findings
- Relevant negative findings
- Comparison with prior studies (if available)

**Organizational Methods:**
- Organ-by-organ (for abdomen/pelvis)
- Region-by-region (for chest)
- System-by-system (for spine)
- Compartment-by-compartment (for musculoskeletal)

**7. Impression/Conclusion**
- Summary of key findings
- Diagnosis or differential diagnosis
- Answers to clinical questions
- Level of concern or urgency
- Comparison with priors (improved, stable, worsened)
- Recommendations for further imaging or clinical management
- Concise and clear (usually a numbered list)

Example:
```
IMPRESSION:
1. 3.2 cm enhancing mass in the right frontal lobe with surrounding vasogenic
   edema, most consistent with high-grade glioma. Metastasis cannot be excluded.
   Clinical correlation and tissue sampling recommended.
2. No acute intracranial hemorrhage or herniation.
3. Recommend neurosurgical consultation.
```

**8. Critical Value Communication**
- Urgent or unexpected findings requiring immediate action
- Documentation of direct communication with the ordering physician
- Time, date, and recipient of verbal communication
- Example: "Critical result: Acute pulmonary embolism. Dr. Smith paged at 14:35 on [date]."

### Structured Reporting Systems

#### Lung-RADS (Lung CT Screening Reporting and Data System)

Used for the interpretation of lung cancer screening CTs.

**Categories:**
- **Lung-RADS 0**: Incomplete - Needs additional imaging
- **Lung-RADS 1**: Negative - No nodules, definitely benign nodules
- **Lung-RADS 2**: Benign appearance or behavior - Nodules with very low likelihood of malignancy
- **Lung-RADS 3**: Probably benign - Short-term follow-up recommended
- **Lung-RADS 4A**: Suspicious - 3-month follow-up or PET/CT
- **Lung-RADS 4B**: Highly suspicious - 3-month follow-up or PET/CT, consider biopsy
- **Lung-RADS 4X**: Highly suspicious with additional features, consider biopsy

**Each category includes management recommendations.**

#### BI-RADS (Breast Imaging-Reporting and Data System)

Standardized lexicon for breast imaging (mammography, ultrasound, MRI).

**Categories:**
- **BI-RADS 0**: Incomplete - Needs additional imaging
- **BI-RADS 1**: Negative - No abnormalities
- **BI-RADS 2**: Benign findings
- **BI-RADS 3**: Probably benign - Short-term follow-up (6 months)
- **BI-RADS 4**: Suspicious - Biopsy recommended
  - 4A: Low suspicion
  - 4B: Moderate suspicion
  - 4C: High suspicion
- **BI-RADS 5**: Highly suggestive of malignancy - Biopsy recommended
- **BI-RADS 6**: Known biopsy-proven malignancy

**Descriptors:**
- Mass: Shape, margin, density
- Calcification: Morphology, distribution
- Asymmetry: Type and characteristics
- Associated features

#### LI-RADS (Liver Imaging-Reporting and Data System)

Used for reporting liver observations in patients at risk for hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC).

**Categories:**
- **LI-RADS 1**: Definitely benign
- **LI-RADS 2**: Probably benign
- **LI-RADS 3**: Intermediate probability of malignancy
- **LI-RADS 4**: Probable HCC
- **LI-RADS 5**: Definite HCC
- **LI-RADS M**: Probable or definite malignancy, not HCC specific
- **LI-RADS TIV**: Tumor in vein

**Major Features Evaluated:**
- Size
- Enhancement pattern (arterial phase hyperenhancement, washout)
- Capsule appearance
- Threshold growth

#### PI-RADS (Prostate Imaging-Reporting and Data System)

Used for multiparametric MRI of the prostate.

**Assessment Categories:**
- **PI-RADS 1**: Very low - Clinically significant cancer is highly unlikely
- **PI-RADS 2**: Low - Clinically significant cancer is unlikely
- **PI-RADS 3**: Intermediate - Presence of clinically significant cancer is equivocal
- **PI-RADS 4**: High - Clinically significant cancer is likely
- **PI-RADS 5**: Very high - Clinically significant cancer is highly likely

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- Peripheral Zone: DWI/ADC is the primary determinant
- Transition Zone: T2-weighted is the primary determinant
- DCE (Dynamic Contrast Enhanced): Used for Peripheral Zone PI-RADS 3 lesions

### RadLex and Standardized Terminology

**RadLex** is a comprehensive radiology lexicon developed by the Radiological Society of North America (RSNA).

**Advantages:**
- Standardization of terminology
- Improved communication
- Support for data mining and analysis
- Facilitation of clinical decision support systems
- Unified report structure

**Common RadLex Terms:**
- Anatomical structures
- Imaging observations
- Disease entities
- Examination procedures

### Radiologic Measurements

**Linear Measurements:**
- Use bidirectional (length × width) or tridimensional (length × width × height) measurements
- Report the maximum diameter of nodules/masses
- Maintain consistent measurement methods during follow-up
- Perform perpendicular measurements whenever possible

**Volumetric Measurements:**
- More accurate for follow-up of irregular lesions
- Use automated or semi-automated software
- Particularly applicable to lung nodules

**Response Assessment:**
- RECIST 1.1 (Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors)
  - Target lesions: Sum of longest diameters (up to 5 lesions, 2 per organ)
  - Complete Response (CR), Partial Response (PR), Stable Disease (SD), Progressive Disease (PD)

## Pathology Reporting Standards

### College of American Pathologists (CAP) Protocols

CAP Cancer Protocols provide standardized synoptic reporting templates for cancer specimens.

#### Synoptic Reporting Elements

**Core elements for all cancer specimens:**

**1. Specimen Information**
- Procedure type (biopsy, resection, radical surgery)
- Specimen laterality
- Specimen integrity and adequacy

**2. Tumor Site**
- Anatomical site and subsite
- Precise location within the organ

**3. Tumor Size**
- Maximum diameter in centimeters
- Additional dimensions if 3D measurement is relevant
- Measurement method (gross vs. microscopic)

**4. Histologic Type**
- WHO classification
- Specific subtypes
- Percentage of components in mixed tumors

**5. Histologic Grade**
- Grading system used (e.g., Nottingham, Fuhrman, Gleason)
- Grade category (well, moderately, poorly differentiated, or G1, G2, G3)
- Individual component scores where applicable

**6. Extent of Invasion**
- Depth of invasion (measured in millimeters)
- Involvement of adjacent structures
- Lymphovascular invasion (present/not identified)
- Perineural invasion (present/not identified)

**7. Margins**
- Distance to the closest margin
- Status of each evaluated margin (negative/positive)
- If positive, specify the involved margin

**8. Lymph Nodes**
- Number of lymph nodes examined
- Number of lymph nodes with metastases
- Size of the largest metastatic deposit
- Extranodal extension (present/absent)

**9. Pathologic Staging (pTNM)**
- pT: Extent of primary tumor
- pN: Regional lymph nodes
- pM: Distant metastasis (if known)
- Version of the AJCC Cancer Staging Manual used

**10. Additional Findings**
- Treatment effect (if post-neoadjuvant therapy)
- Associated lesions (dysplasia, carcinoma in situ)
- Background tissue (cirrhosis, inflammation)

**11. Ancillary Studies**
- Immunohistochemistry results
- Molecular/genetic testing results
- Biomarker status (e.g., ER, PR, HER2 for breast cancer; MSI for colon cancer)
- FISH or other cytogenetic results

#### Organ-Specific CAP Protocols

**Breast Cancer:**
- Histologic type (Invasive ductal, lobular, special types)
- Nottingham grade (tubule formation, nuclear pleomorphism, mitotic count)
- ER/PR status (percentage and intensity)
- HER2 status (IHC score, FISH if necessary)
- Ki-67 proliferation index
- DCIS component (if present)
- Neoadjuvant treatment response (Residual Cancer Burden)

**Colorectal Cancer:**
- Histologic type (Adenocarcinoma, mucinous, etc.)
- Grade
- Depth of invasion (into submucosa, muscularis propria, pericolic tissue, etc.)
- Tumor deposits
- Lymph nodes (positive/total examined)
- Margins (proximal, distal, radial/circumferential)
- MSI/MMR status
- KRAS, NRAS, BRAF mutations

**Prostate Cancer:**
- Gleason score (primary + secondary patterns)
- Grade group (1-5)
- Percentage of tissue involvement
- Extraprostatic extension
- Seminal vesicle invasion
- Surgical margin status
- Lymph nodes (if sampled)

**Lung Cancer:**
- Histologic type (Adenocarcinoma, squamous cell, small cell, etc.)
- Grade (for NSCLC)
- Depth of invasion
- Visceral pleural invasion
- Distance to margins
- Lymph nodes
- Molecular markers (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, PD-L1)

### Pathology Gross Description

**Essential Elements:**
- Specimen label and identification
- Specimen type
- Dimensions and weight
- Orientation markers (if present)
- Description of the external surface
- Appearance of the cut surface
- Description of the lesion:
  - Size (3 dimensions)
  - Location
  - Color
  - Consistency
  - Borders (well-circumscribed, infiltrative)
  - Distance to margins
- Sampling method (how tissue was sectioned and submitted)

**Example:**
```
GROSS DESCRIPTION:
Received fresh, labeled with patient name and "left breast, lumpectomy" is an
oriented lumpectomy specimen measuring 8.5 x 6.0 x 4.0 cm, with a suture
indicating superior margin. Inking: superior - blue, inferior - black, medial -
green, lateral - red, anterior - orange, posterior - yellow. Serially sectioned
to reveal a firm, gray-white mass measuring 2.1 x 1.8 x 1.5 cm, located 2.5 cm
from superior, 3.0 cm from inferior, 2.0 cm from medial, 3.5 cm from lateral,
1.5 cm from anterior, and 1.8 cm from posterior margins. Representative sections
submitted as follows: A1-A3 tumor, A4 superior margin, A5 medial margin, A6
posterior margin.
```

### Microscopic Description

**Key Elements:**
- Architectural patterns
- Cellular features
  - Cell type
  - Nuclear features (size, shape, chromatin, nucleoli)
  - Cytoplasmic features
  - Mitotic activity
- Degree of differentiation
- Pattern of invasion
- Special features (necrosis, hemorrhage, calcification)
- Stroma and background tissue
- Lymphovascular or perineural invasion
- Margins (distance and status)
- Lymph nodes (description of metastases)

### Intraoperative Frozen Section Report

**Indications:**
- Intraoperative diagnosis
- Margin assessment
- Lymph node assessment
- Tissue triaging

**Reporting Format:**
- Clearly labeled "Frozen Section Diagnosis"
- Intraoperative consultation record
- Time of frozen section
- Specimen description
- Frozen section diagnosis
- Note: "Final diagnosis pending paraffin sections"

**Frozen Section Disclaimer:**
- Limited by freezing artifacts
- Final diagnosis based on paraffin-embedded sections
- Definitive diagnosis deferred to paraffin sections

### Diagnostic Certainty Terminology

**Definite:**
- "Consistent with..."
- "Diagnostic of..."
- "Positive for..."

**Probable:**
- "Consistent with..."
- "Favor..."
- "Most likely..."

**Possible:**
- "Suggestive of..."
- "Cannot exclude..."
- "Differential diagnosis includes..."

**Deferred:**
- "Defer to..."
- "Recommend..."
- "Additional studies pending..."

## Laboratory Reporting Standards

### Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI) Guidelines

CLSI provides standards for laboratory testing and reporting.

#### Components of a Laboratory Report

**1. Patient Demographics**
- Patient name and identifier
- Date of birth or age
- Gender
- Ordering physician

**2. Specimen Information**
- Specimen type (blood, serum, plasma, urine, CSF, etc.)
- Collection date and time
- Receipt date and time
- Specimen condition
- Fasting status (if relevant)

**3. Test Information**
- Test name (full name, not just abbreviation)
- Test code
- Methodology
- Accession or specimen number

**4. Results**
- Quantitative values with units
- Qualitative results (positive/negative, detected/not detected)
- Reference ranges or intervals
- Abnormal result flags
  - H = High
  - L = Low
  - Highlighting critical or panic values

**5. Reference Intervals**
- Age-specific
- Gender-specific
- Population-specific (when relevant)
- Method-specific
- Clear units

**Example:**
```
Test: Hemoglobin A1c
Result: 8.2%  (H)
Reference Range: 4.0-5.6% (non-diabetic)
Method: HPLC
Interpretation: Consistent with poorly controlled diabetes
```

**6. Interpretive Comments**
- When results require context
- Suggestions for additional testing
- Explanation of interfering factors or limitations
- Clinical guidance

**7. Quality Control**
- Delta checks (comparison with previous values)
- Critical value read-back procedures
- Specimen quality issues (hemolysis, lipemia, icterus)
- Dilutions performed
- Repeat testing if necessary

### LOINC (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes)

A standard coding system for laboratory and clinical observations.

**LOINC Code Components:**
- Component (Analyte measured)
- Property (Mass, substance concentration, etc.)
- Time (Point in time, 24 hours)
- System (Specimen type)
- Scale (Quantitative, nominal, ordinal)
- Method (When relevant)

**Examples:**
- Hemoglobin A1c in blood: 4548-4
- Glucose in serum/plasma: 2345-7
- Creatinine in serum/plasma: 2160-0

### Critical Value Reporting

**Definition:** Test results that indicate a life-threatening situation requiring immediate clinical action.

**Examples of Critical Values:**
- Glucose: <40 mg/dL or >500 mg/dL
- Potassium: <2.5 mEq/L or >6.5 mEq/L
- Sodium: <120 mEq/L or >160 mEq/L
- Calcium: <6.0 mg/dL or >13.0 mg/dL
- WBC count: <1.0 × 10³/μL or >50 × 10³/μL
- Hemoglobin: <5.0 g/dL
- Platelets: <20 × 10³/μL
- INR: >5.0 (on warfarin)
- Positive blood culture
- Positive CSF culture or Gram stain

**Critical Value Procedure:**
1. Laboratory identifies the result
2. Immediate contact with the ordering physician or designated personnel
3. Read-back verification
4. Documentation:
   - Date and time
   - Person contacted
   - Person receiving notification
   - Test and result
5. Follow hospital policy if the physician cannot be reached

### Microbiology Reports

**Culture Reports:**
- Specimen type and source
- Organisms identified
- Quantity (scant, moderate, heavy growth)
- Antimicrobial susceptibility results
- Interpretation (Susceptible, Intermediate, Resistant)
- MIC (Minimum Inhibitory Concentration) values when applicable

**Gram Stain Reports:**
- Bacteria present (Gram-positive/negative, morphology)
- Quantity and cellular background
- Presence of WBCs or other cells

**Preliminary Reports:**
- Issued before final identification is complete
- Clearly marked "PRELIMINARY"
- Followed by a final report

**Final Reports:**
- Definitive organism identification
- Complete susceptibility panel
- Interpretive comments

### Molecular Pathology/Genomics Reports

**Components:**
- Genes tested
- Variants detected
- Classification (Pathogenic, Likely Pathogenic, VUS, Likely Benign, Benign)
- Allele frequency
- Methodology (NGS, Sanger sequencing, PCR, etc.)
- Reference sequence
- Clinical significance and interpretation
- Recommendations (therapeutic implications, family testing)
- Test limitations

**Example:**
```
Test: BRCA1/BRCA2 Full Gene Sequencing
Result: PATHOGENIC VARIANT DETECTED
Gene: BRCA1
Variant: c.68_69delAG (p.Glu23ValfsTer17)
Classification: Pathogenic
Interpretation: This variant is associated with increased risk of breast and
ovarian cancer. Genetic counseling and risk-reducing strategies recommended.
Family testing should be considered.
```

### Point-of-Care Testing (POCT)

**Requirements:**
- Same quality standards as the central laboratory
- Documentation of operator competency
- Quality control records
- Maintenance records
- Documentation of results in the medical record

**Common POCT:**
- Blood glucose
- Hemoglobin/Hematocrit
- INR
- Blood gases
- Pregnancy tests
- Urinalysis
- Rapid Strep tests
- Influenza tests

## Diagnostic Reporting Quality Indicators

### Radiology Quality Indicators

- Report turnaround time (routine vs. urgent)
- Critical value communication time
- Report error rate
- Addendum rate
- Referring physician satisfaction

**Benchmarks:**
- Routine reports: <24 hours
- Urgent reports: <4 hours
- STAT reports: <1 hour
- Critical findings: Immediate verbal communication

### Pathology Quality Indicators

- Turnaround Time (TAT) by specimen type
- Frozen section accuracy rate
- Amended report rate
- Specimen adequacy rate
- Immunohistochemistry quality control

**TAT Benchmarks:**
- Routine surgical pathology: 2-3 days
- Complex surgical pathology: 5-7 days
- Cytology: 1-2 days
- Intraoperative frozen section: 15-20 minutes

### Laboratory Quality Indicators

- TAT from collection to result
- Critical value notification time
- Specimen rejection rate
- Proficiency testing performance
- Delta check failure rate

**TAT Benchmarks:**
- STAT labs: <60 minutes
- Routine labs: 2-4 hours
- Send-out tests: Dependent on reference laboratory
