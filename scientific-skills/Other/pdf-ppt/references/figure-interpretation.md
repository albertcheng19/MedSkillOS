# Figure and Table Interpretation Rules

Use these rules when interpreting Results, figure legends, and tables for the PPT.

## Scope and Ordering

- Use Results as the primary source; fill gaps with figure legends.
- Ignore supplementary data (e.g., Figure S1, S2) unless explicitly requested.
- Interpret figures and tables in the same order as the Results section.
- Cover all figures (including mechanism diagrams) and all tables.

## Figure/Subfigure Mapping

- Start each paragraph with the exact subfigure label(s), e.g., "# D, F".
- If multiple subfigures support one conclusion, merge them (e.g., "A-C" as a single paragraph).
- Ensure every paragraph maps to the corresponding image(s) used on the slide.

## Content Completeness

- Every paragraph must include: experimental purpose, method/assay name, analysis approach, and concrete results.
- Describe directionality explicitly (increase/decrease, up/down), avoid vague wording.
- Distinguish experimental vs control groups clearly.
- Avoid incomplete statements; include the specific treatment or condition.
- Integrate necessary background context into the relevant subfigure paragraph, but keep it concise.
- Do not use filler phrases such as "as shown in the figure."

## Style Requirements

- Write in professional, concise Chinese.
- Use complete sentences; avoid literal translation or awkward phrasing.

## Sentence Patterns (use in Chinese)

- Purpose-driven: "To [purpose], use [assay] on [object]; results show [outcome]."
- Method-led: "Using [assay], to [purpose], results indicate [outcome]."
- Direct statement: "[Assay] analysis shows [outcome], supporting [purpose]."

## Critical Appraisal (final slide)

Focus on core contributions and internal limitations (no abstract-style restatement).

- Core strengths: theory novelty, method innovation, perspective shift, potential impact.
- Limitations: methodological constraints, logical/theoretical gaps, evidence sufficiency, future risks or overlooked variables.
