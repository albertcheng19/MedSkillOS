# Research Grants Skill

## Overview

This is a comprehensive skill for writing competitive research grant proposals, focusing on the four major US funding agencies:
- **NSF** (National Science Foundation)
- **NIH** (National Institutes of Health)
- **DOE** (Department of Energy)
- **DARPA** (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)

## What This Skill Provides

### Agency-Specific Guidance

Detailed reference materials for each funding agency, including:
- Mission and priorities
- Review criteria and scoring
- Proposal structure and page limits
- Budget requirements
- Submission processes
- Tips for competitive applications

### Core Components

- **Specific Aims Pages** (NIH): Templates and detailed guides for the critical 1-page aims document.
- **Project Summaries** (NSF): Templates including the required "Overview," "Intellectual Merit," and "Broader Impacts" sections.
- **Broader Impacts**: Comprehensive strategies for the NSF review criterion of equal weight.
- **Budget Justification**: Templates and examples for personnel, equipment, travel, and materials.
- **Review Criteria**: Insights into what reviewers at each agency look for.

### Templates

Plug-and-play templates, including:
- NSF Project Summary
- NIH Specific Aims Page
- Budget Justifications
- (More templates under development)

## Key Features

### NSF Proposals

- **Intellectual Merit + Broader Impacts** (Equal weight)
- Substantive, measurable Broader Impacts strategies
- Integration of research and education
- Broadening participation in STEM fields
- 15-page Project Description limit (for most programs)

### NIH Proposals

- **Specific Aims Page**: The most important page (includes a detailed 1-page guide)
- **Research Strategy**: Significance, Innovation, and Approach sections
- **Preliminary Data**: Crucial for R01 applications
- Rigor and Reproducibility requirements
- Modular Budget vs. Detailed Budget
- Resubmission strategies (A1 applications)

### DOE Proposals

- **Energy Relevance** and alignment with the DOE mission
- **Technology Readiness Levels** (TRLs)
- National Laboratory collaborations
- Cost-sharing requirements (especially for ARPA-E)
- Commercialization pathways
- Use of User Facilities

### DARPA Proposals

- **DARPA-hard problems**: High-risk, high-reward
- **Heilmeier Catechism**: 8 key questions
- Program Manager engagement (Critical!)
- Phased structures with milestones
- Tech transition plans
- Demonstrations and prototyping

## Success Metrics

Typical success rates by agency:

- **DOE Office of Science**: 20-40% (varies by program)

- **DARPA**: Varies significantly by program

## Best Practices

### Start Early
- NSF/NIH Proposals: Start 3-6 months before the deadline
- DOE/DARPA Proposals: 4-6 months (especially if National Labs are involved)

### Get Feedback
- Mock review sessions
- Colleagues within and outside your field
- Institutional Grant Support Offices
- Program Officers (where appropriate)

### Understand Review Criteria
- NSF: Intellectual Merit + Broader Impacts (Equal weight)
- NIH: Significance, Investigator, Innovation, Approach, Environment (Scored 1-9)
- DOE: Technical Merit, Qualifications, Budget, Relevance
- DARPA: Innovation, Impact, Team, Feasibility, Transition

### Common Success Factors

✅ Clear and persuasive Significance and Innovation
✅ Strong Preliminary Data (NIH, DOE)
✅ Detailed, rigorous Methodology
✅ Realistic Timeline and Budget
✅ Specific, measurable Outcomes
✅ Strong Team with relevant expertise
✅ Integration of Broader Impacts (NSF)
✅ Tech Transition plans (DOE, DARPA)

## Tips for Maximum Effectiveness

### For NSF Proposals
1. Start with Specific Aims/Objectives (even if not strictly required)
2. Develop Broader Impacts with the same rigor as the research plan
3. Use visuals extensively (make it easy to skim)
4. Explicitly address both review criteria
5. Get feedback from someone outside your immediate field

### For NIH Proposals
1. Perfect your Specific Aims page first (revise 10+ times)
2. Include substantive preliminary data
3. Explicitly address Rigor and Reproducibility
4. Proactively identify potential pitfalls and provide alternatives
5. Ensure your aims are independent yet synergistic

### For DOE Proposals
1. Emphasize energy relevance and impact
2. Include quantitative metrics (cost, efficiency, emissions)
3. Develop a deployment or commercialization pathway
4. Consider partnerships with National Labs
5. Address Technology Readiness Levels (TRL)

### For DARPA Proposals
1. Contact the Program Manager early (Crucial!)
2. Attend Proposers Day events
3. Focus on breakthrough innovation (10x improvement, not 10%)
4. Explicitly answer the Heilmeier Catechism
5. Have a clear transition strategy
