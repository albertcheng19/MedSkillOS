# ISO 13485:2016 Mandatory Documentation and Records List

This document provides a complete list of all mandatory documents and records required by the ISO 13485:2016 Medical Devices Quality Management System (QMS).

## Overview

ISO 13485:2016 requires an organization to establish and maintain **31 procedure documents**, as well as a **Quality Manual** and a **Medical Device File**. Additionally, extensive **records** must be maintained to provide evidence of conformity.

**Important Notes:**
- 31 procedure documents do not necessarily require 31 separate files.
- Multiple procedures can be combined into a single document.
- A single procedure can be split into multiple documents.
- Not all procedures apply to every organization (justification must be provided for non-applicable items).
- Applicable regulatory requirements may necessitate additional documentation.

## 1. Quality Manual (Requirement 4.2.2)

**Description:** The foundational document of the QMS.

**Must Include:**
- The scope of the QMS, including justifications for any exclusions.
- Documented procedures or references to them.
- A description of the interaction between QMS processes.
- The structure of the documentation used in the QMS.

**Applicable to:** All organizations.

---

## 2. Medical Device File (Requirement 4.2.3)

**Description:** Documentation for each medical device model or type/family.

**Must Include:**
- General description and intended use.
- Specifications for labeling and instructions for use (IFU).
- Product and/or manufacturing specifications.
- Procedures for purchasing, manufacturing, and servicing.
- Procedures for measurement and monitoring.
- Installation requirements (if applicable).
- Risk management documentation.
- Verification and validation information.
- Design and development documentation (if applicable).

**Applicable to:** All organizations; must be prepared for each device model/family.

## 3. The 31 Procedure Documents

### Chapter 4: Quality Management System

#### 1. Risk Management Procedure (4.1.5)
**Description:** Risk management requirements throughout product realization.
**Must Cover:**
- Risk management methodology.
- Risk analysis and evaluation.
- Risk control measures.
- Risk acceptance criteria.
- Risk management review.
**Reference Standard:** ISO 14971.

#### 2. Software Validation Procedure (4.1.6)
**Description:** Validation of computer software applications used in the QMS.
**Must Cover:**
- Software risk assessment.
- Validation methods and activities.
- Acceptance criteria.
- User responsibilities.
- Validation before first use and after changes.
- Re-validation criteria.

#### 3. Document Control Procedure (4.2.4)
**Description:** Control of all QMS documents.
**Must Cover:**
- Approval of documents before issue.
- Review and update of documents.
- Identification of changes and revision status.
- Availability of relevant document versions.
- Document legibility and identification.
- Control of external documents.
- Prevention of unintended use of obsolete documents.
- Identification of retained obsolete documents.

#### 4. Record Control Procedure (4.2.5)
**Description:** Control of all QMS records.
**Must Cover:**
- Record identification.
- Storage and security.
- Integrity protection.
- Retrieval procedures.
- Determination of retention periods.
- Disposal of records.
- Legibility requirements.
- Identification of changes.

### Chapter 5: Management Responsibility

#### 5. Management Review Procedure (5.6.1)
**Description:** Systematic review of the QMS by top management.
**Must Cover:**
- Review frequency (at least annually).
- Review agenda and inputs.
- Review outputs and actions.
- Attendee requirements.
- Record-keeping requirements.

#### 6. Internal Communication Procedure (5.5.3)
**Description:** Communication regarding the effectiveness of the QMS.
**Must Cover:**
- Communication channels.
- Communication frequency.
- Responsible parties.
- Communication topics.
- Archiving of communication records.

### Chapter 6: Resource Management

#### 7. Human Resources/Competence Procedure (6.2)
**Description:** Ensuring personnel competence.
**Must Cover:**
- Determination of competence requirements.
- Identification of training needs.
- Provision and evaluation of training.
- Requirements for education, training, skills, and experience.
- Maintenance of competence records.
- Employee awareness of their contribution.

#### 8. Infrastructure Maintenance Procedure (6.3)
**Description:** Maintenance of facilities and equipment (when it affects quality).
**Must Cover:**
- Definition of maintenance activities.
- Maintenance scheduling.
- Maintenance records.
- Impact on product quality.
- Verification after maintenance.

#### 9. Contamination Control Procedure (6.4.2)
**Description:** Control of contaminated or potentially contaminated products (if applicable).
**Must Cover:**
- Contamination identification.
- Handling of contaminated products.
- Segregation requirements.
- Decontamination procedures.
- Special control arrangements.

### Chapter 7: Product Realization

#### 10. Customer Communication Procedure (7.2.3)
**Description:** Communication with customers.
**Must Cover:**
- Provision of product information.
- Handling of inquiries, contracts, and orders.
- Customer feedback (including complaints).
- Advisory notices.
- Communication channels and responsibilities.

#### 11. Design and Development Procedure (7.3.1 - 7.3.10)
**Description:** Control of the design and development process.
**Must Cover:**
- Design and development planning.
- Determination and review of inputs.
- Provision and approval of outputs.
- Design reviews at appropriate stages.
- Verification against inputs.
- Validation against intended use.
- Design transfer to manufacturing.
- Change control.
- Maintenance of design documentation.

#### 12. Purchasing Procedure (7.4.1)
**Description:** Control of the purchasing process.
**Must Cover:**
- Criteria for supplier evaluation and selection.
- Supplier monitoring and re-evaluation.
- Supplier performance tracking.
- Purchasing controls.
- Supplier change notification.
- Communication with sub-tier suppliers.

#### 13. Verification of Purchased Product Procedure (7.4.3)
**Description:** Verifying that purchased products meet requirements.
**Must Cover:**
- Verification activities.
- Extent of verification.
- Product release methods.
- Verification at supplier premises (if applicable).
- Customer verification arrangements (if applicable).

#### 14. Control of Production and Service Provision Procedure (7.5.1)
**Description:** Control of production and service activities.
**Must Cover:**
- Work instructions and procedure documents.
- Use of suitable equipment.
- Monitoring and measurement activities.
- Release, delivery, and post-delivery activities.
- Labeling and packaging operations.
- Service procedures (if applicable).

#### 15. Product Cleanliness Procedure (7.5.2)
**Description:** Control of product cleanliness (if applicable).
**Must Cover:**
- Cleanliness requirements and specifications.
- Cleaning prior to sterilization.
- Handling of non-cleanable products.
- Hygiene requirements during manufacturing.
- Cleaning validation.

#### 16. Installation and Verification Procedure (7.5.3)
**Description:** Installation activities (if applicable).
**Must Cover:**
- Installation requirements.
- Installation instructions.
- Installation verification.
- Installation records.
- Responsible parties.

#### 17. Servicing Procedure (7.5.4)
**Description:** Servicing activities (if applicable).
**Must Cover:**
- Servicing requirements.
- Reference materials and measurements.
- Analysis of service feedback.
- Servicing records.
- Issuing service notices to regulatory authorities.

#### 18. Process Validation Procedure (7.5.6)
**Description:** Validation of processes where output cannot be verified.
**Must Cover:**
- Manufacturing process validation.
- Validation of computer software for production.
- Sterilization process validation.
- Sterile processing validation.
- Cleanroom validation (if applicable).
- Review and approval criteria.
- Equipment approval and personnel qualification.
- Re-validation criteria and triggers.

#### 19. Sterilization and Sterile Barrier System Validation (7.5.7)
**Description:** Validation of sterilization processes (if applicable).
**Must Cover:**
- Sterilization method validation.
- Sterile barrier system validation.
- Process parameters for each batch.
- Validation of sterilization changes.
- Maintenance of validation records.

#### 20. Product Identification and Traceability Procedure (7.5.8, 7.5.9)
**Description:** Identification and traceability throughout realization.
**Must Cover:**
- Identification methods during realization.
- Definition of traceability scope.
- Distribution and location tracking.
- Consignee identification.
- Identification of shipped quantities.
- Retention period requirements.
- Applicable regulatory traceability requirements.

#### 21. Customer Property Procedure (7.5.10)
**Description:** Control of property provided by customers.
**Must Cover:**
- Customer property identification.
- Customer property verification.
- Protection and maintenance.
- Reporting of loss, damage, or unsuitability.
- Customer property records.

#### 22. Preservation of Product Procedure (7.5.11)
**Description:** Preservation of product during processing and delivery.
**Must Cover:**
- Identification requirements.
- Handling requirements.
- Packaging requirements.
- Storage requirements.
- Protection requirements.
- Special handling (if applicable).
- Applicability to components.

#### 23. Control of Monitoring and Measuring Equipment Procedure (7.6)
**Description:** Calibration and control of measuring equipment.
**Must Cover:**
- Equipment identification.
- Calibration or verification intervals.
- Calibration methods and standards.
- Adjustment procedures.
- Calibration status identification.
- Safeguarding against invalidating adjustments.
- Protection from damage.
- Assessment when found out of specification.
- Computer software validation.

### Chapter 8: Measurement, Analysis, and Improvement

#### 24. Feedback Procedure (8.2.1)
**Description:** Post-production information system.
**Must Cover:**
- Early warning system for quality problems.
- Collection of post-production information.
- Linkage to complaint handling.
- Reporting to regulatory authorities.
- Input to risk management.
- Input to corrective and preventive actions.
- Feedback sources and channels.

#### 25. Complaint Handling Procedure (8.2.2)
**Description:** Timely handling of complaints.
**Must Cover:**
- Receiving and recording complaints.
- Evaluating and investigating complaints.
- Determining the need to report to authorities.
- Determining the need for advisory notices.
- Communicating actions taken to customers.
- Transferring complaint information not handled directly.
- Complaint trend analysis.

#### 26. Reporting to Regulatory Authorities Procedure (8.2.3)
**Description:** Notification of regulatory authorities.
**Must Cover:**
- Determination of reportable events.
- Notification timelines.
- Notification content requirements.
- Advisory notice requirements.
- Regional applicable regulatory requirements.
- Responsibility for reporting.

#### 27. Internal Audit Procedure (8.2.4)
**Description:** Implementation of internal audits.
**Must Cover:**
- Audit program planning.
- Audit criteria, scope, frequency, and methods.
- Auditor selection and impartiality.
- Audit responsibilities and requirements.
- Audit reporting.
- Audit records.
- Follow-up activities.

#### 28. Monitoring and Measurement of Processes Procedure (8.2.5)
**Description:** Monitoring and measurement of QMS processes.
**Must Cover:**
- Process monitoring methods.
- Process measurement methods.
- Demonstration of the ability to achieve planned results.
- Implementation of corrections when necessary.
- Corrective actions for process failures.

#### 29. Monitoring and Measurement of Product Procedure (8.2.6)
**Description:** Monitoring and measurement of product.
**Must Cover:**
- Product acceptance criteria.
- Measurement at appropriate stages.
- Identification of release authorization.
- Release procedures.
- Records of conformity to criteria.
- Traceability of measurement authorization.

#### 30. Control of Nonconforming Product Procedure (8.3)
**Description:** Identification and control of nonconforming products.
**Must Cover:**
- Identification of nonconformities.
- Documentation requirements.
- Evaluation of nonconformities.
- Segregation of nonconforming products.
- Disposition of nonconforming products.
- Notification of external parties.
- Concession process (if applicable).
- Rework procedures.
- Actions for nonconformities found before delivery.
- Actions for nonconformities found after delivery.
- Reporting requirements.

#### 31A. Corrective Action Procedure (8.5.2)
**Description:** Eliminating causes of nonconformities.
**Must Cover:**
- Reviewing nonconformities (including complaints).
- Methods for determining causes.
- Evaluating the need for action.
- Planning and recording actions.
- Implementation of actions.
- Review of action effectiveness.
- Records of investigations and follow-up.

#### 31B. Preventive Action Procedure (8.5.3)
**Description:** Eliminating causes of potential nonconformities.
**Must Cover:**
- Identifying potential nonconformities.
- Evaluating the need for action.
- Planning and recording actions.
- Implementation of actions.
- Review of action effectiveness.
- Information sources for preventive actions.
- Records of investigations and follow-up.

## Other Required Documents

### Data Analysis Procedure (8.4)
While a "procedure document" is not explicitly mandated by name, the organization must establish processes for:
- Determination, collection, and analysis of data.
- Evaluation of opportunities for continuous improvement.
- Statistical techniques (if applicable).
- Analysis content including:
  - Customer satisfaction.
  - Conformity to product requirements.
  - Characteristics and trends of processes and products.
  - Suppliers.
  - Other relevant data (including feedback and risk management outputs).

## Document Management Tips

### Merging Procedures
Multiple procedures can be merged into a single document, for example:
- "Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) Procedure" (merging 8.5.2 and 8.5.3)
- "Document and Record Control Procedure" (merging 4.2.4 and 4.2.5)
- "Monitoring and Measurement Procedure" (merging 8.2.5 and 8.2.6)
- "Product Identification and Traceability Procedure" (merging 7.5.8 and 7.5.9)

### Determining Applicability
Not all procedures apply to all organizations. Common exclusions include:
- Design and Development (when manufacturing only to customer specifications)
- Installation (when the product does not require installation)
- Servicing (when servicing is not provided)
- Sterilization (when the product is non-sterile)
- Contamination Control (when not applicable to the product type)

All exclusions must be justified in the Quality Manual.

### Regulatory Requirements
Remember that applicable regulatory requirements may mandate additional documentation beyond ISO 13485, including:
- FDA Regulations (21 CFR Part 820 / QMSR)
- EU MDR/IVDR requirements
- Health Canada requirements
- Other regional regulatory requirements

## Record Retention

According to ISO 13485:2016 (4.2.5), records must be retained for:
- **Minimum:** The lifetime of the medical device as defined by the organization.
- **Additional:** Not less than the retention period of any resulting record.
- **Regulatory:** As specified by applicable regulatory requirements.

The organization must define the "lifetime" of its medical devices and establish retention times that meet or exceed:
- ISO 13485 minimum requirements.
- Applicable regulatory requirements (often 5-10 years minimum).
- Any customer contract requirements.

---

## Transition to the Medical Device File (MDF)

With the advancement of FDA QMSR harmonization (effective February 2, 2026), organizations should prepare to transition separate files into a unified Medical Device File (MDF) to replace:
- **DHF** (Design History File)
- **DMR** (Device Master Record)
- **DHR** (Device History Record)

The MDF approach aligns with ISO 13485:2016 requirements and provides a more unified documentation structure.
