# Specialized Evaluation — Category 4: Academic Writing

This rubric contributes up to **60 points** to the per-output total score.

| **Core Specialized Dimension** | **Evaluation Focus & Risk Avoidance** | **Scoring Rubric** | **Max** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Terminology Precision** | **Medical / professional rigor**: accurate terminology, strict separation of association vs. causation, no exaggeration. | **16–20 pts**: Wording is highly precise and fully consistent with professional norms; **10–15 pts**: Terminology is broadly correct but minor contextual errors exist; **0–9 pts**: Terminology errors are pervasive and conclusions are severely overstated. | 20 |
| **2. Scientific Logic** | **Reasoning chain**: rigor of the mechanism→phenotype and evidence→conclusion derivations. | **12–15 pts**: Logic is seamless and argumentation is flawless; **8–11 pts**: Logic is broadly sound but argumentation force is slightly weak or has leaps; **0–7 pts**: Logical gaps exist and evidence cannot support the conclusions. | 15 |
| **3. Evidence & Design Understanding** | **Background depth**: whether understanding of evidence hierarchy and experimental design is reflected in the written text. | **12–15 pts**: Descriptive depth meets high-tier article standards with superb detail control; **8–11 pts**: Understanding is adequate but lacks presentation of complex design details; **0–7 pts**: Experimental design or evidence hierarchy is completely misstated. | 15 |
| **4. Academic Tone** | **Objective neutrality**: consistent with SCI context, avoids marketing and subjective expression. | **8–10 pts**: Tone is objective, restrained, and neutral with a high-level academic feel; **5–7 pts**: Tone is broadly appropriate with occasional colloquial or non-standard phrasing; **0–4 pts**: Full of subjective color or obvious "AI / marketing" register. | 10 |
| **Total** | **Direct usability and quality grade of the Skill's academic text output** | — | **60** |
