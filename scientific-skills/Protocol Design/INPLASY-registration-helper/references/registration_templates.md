# INPLASY Registration Prompt Templates

Use these templates to generate the respective sections of the INPLASY registration form.

1. Title
The effectiveness of [intervention] compared with [comparator] for [disease/condition] in [population]: a systematic review and meta-analysis protocol
￼
2. Review question / Objective
To systematically evaluate the effects of [intervention] compared with [comparator]on [primary outcomes] in patients with [disease/condition], based on randomized controlled trials.
￼
3. Eligibility criteria
Types of studies
Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) will be included.
Types of participants
Patients diagnosed with [disease/condition], regardless of age, sex, ethnicity, or disease severity.
Types of interventions
The intervention group will receive [intervention].
Types of comparators
The control group will receive [placebo / usual care / other intervention].
Types of outcomes
Primary outcomes: [e.g., overall survival, progression-free survival, pain score, functional score]
Secondary outcomes: [e.g., adverse events, quality of life]
￼
4. Information sources
The following databases will be searched: PubMed, Embase, the Cochrane Library, Web of Science, and other relevant databases if applicable.
￼
5. Search strategy
A comprehensive search strategy will be applied using combinations of Medical Subject Headings (MeSH) terms and free-text words related to [disease/condition]and [intervention].
No language restrictions will be applied.
￼
6. Study selection
Two reviewers will independently screen titles and abstracts, followed by full-text assessment according to the eligibility criteria. Any disagreements will be resolved through discussion or consultation with a third reviewer.
￼
7. Data extraction
Two reviewers will independently extract data using a standardized data extraction form, including study characteristics, participant characteristics, interventions, outcomes, and risk of bias information.
￼
8. Risk of bias assessment
The risk of bias of included randomized controlled trials will be assessed using the Cochrane Risk of Bias tool.
￼
9. Data synthesis
Meta-analysis will be conducted using RevMan or Stata software.
For dichotomous outcomes, risk ratios (RRs) with 95% confidence intervals (CIs) will be calculated.
For continuous outcomes, mean differences (MDs) or standardized mean differences (SMDs) with 95% CIs will be used.
￼
10. Subgroup analysis
Subgroup analyses will be performed according to predefined factors such as age, intervention duration, or disease severity, if sufficient data are available.
￼
11. Sensitivity analysis
Sensitivity analysis will be conducted by excluding studies with a high risk of bias to assess the robustness of the results.
￼
12. Language
No language restriction will be applied.
￼
