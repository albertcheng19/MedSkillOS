# Writing Guide for Each Section of the Paper

## Role Setting

Clinical research expert, specializing in systematic reviews and meta-analyses, skilled in academic writing and synthesizing complex information.

## Abstract

### Structure Requirements
- **Background**: 1-2 sentences, importance of the research question
- **Methods**: Study design, data sources, statistical methods
- **Results**: Key findings and critical statistical data
- **Conclusion**: Main conclusion and clinical significance

### Output Requirements
- 200-300 words
- Define abbreviations at first appearance
- No references needed

---

## Introduction

### Writing Steps

1. **Establish Background**
   - Description of the clinical problem
   - Disease prevalence and impact (global/regional data)

2. **Current Treatment Challenges**
   - Existing treatment methods and limitations
   - Current clinical challenges

3. **Research Gap**
   - Inconsistencies or gaps in the literature
   - Why a meta-analysis is needed

4. **Research Positioning**
   - Research objectives and significance
   - Expected contributions

### Output Requirements
- 300-500 words
- No headings/subheadings
- No more than 10 references, marked as `[PMID: xxxxxxxx]`

---

## Discussion

### Module 1: Summary of Main Findings (Beginning of Discussion)

**Objective**: Summarize key findings, emphasize statistical and clinical significance

**Key Points**:
- Directly state main results
- Provide statistical evidence (HR, CI, p-value)
- Explain clinical relevance

**Word Count**: 150-200 words

### Module 2: Interpretation of Results

**Objective**: Analyze mechanisms and significance of findings in depth

**Key Points**:
- Biological/pathophysiological explanations
- How methodology affects results
- Clinical and biological relevance

**Guiding Questions**:
- What mechanisms explain these findings?
- How are they related to the research background?

**Word Count**: 150+ words

### Module 3: Comparison with Literature

**Objective**: Compare with existing studies, position contributions

**Key Points**:
- Similarities/differences with previous studies
- Analysis of reasons for differences (population, methods, definitions)
- Implications for the field

**Word Count**: 150+ words

### Module 4: Study Limitations

**Objective**: Objectively acknowledge shortcomings

**Common Limitations**:
- Heterogeneity of included studies
- Publication bias
- Quality of original studies
- Control of confounding factors
- Power of subgroup analyses

**Word Count**: 100-150 words

### Module 5: Conclusion and Outlook (End of Discussion)

**Key Points**:
- Restate main conclusions
- Clinical practice recommendations
- Directions for future research

**Word Count**: 100-150 words

### General Requirements for Discussion
- Natural transitions between modules, no subheadings
- No more than 10 references per module
- References marked as `[PMID: xxxxxxxx]`

## Reference Standards

### During Writing
```
...affects patients' response to immunotherapy [PMID: 38887558]...
```

### Final Format
```
...affects patients' response to immunotherapy [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38887558/)...
```

### Reference List
```
[1] Author A, Author B, et al. Title of the article. Journal Name. 2024;Vol(Issue):Pages. [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38887558/]
```
