# Specialty-Specific Treatment Plan Guidelines

## Overview

This reference provides detailed guidelines for developing treatment plans across six template types (General Medicine, Rehabilitation, Mental Health, Chronic Disease Management, Perioperative, and Pain Management). Each section includes specialty-specific considerations, clinical pearls, and best practices.

## Concise Documentation Examples by Specialty

### Basic Medical Model: Concise vs. Verbose

**Principle**: Focus on actionable information; eliminate redundancy; use bullet points and short paragraphs.

### General Medicine - Diabetes Example

**Verbose (Avoid)**:
> "The patient was educated on the pathophysiology of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, including detailed explanations of insulin resistance, pancreatic beta-cell dysfunction, and the progressive nature of the disease. The patient was educated on various potential complications of diabetes, including microvascular complications such as diabetic retinopathy which can lead to blindness, diabetic nephropathy which can progress to end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis, and diabetic neuropathy which can cause pain and loss of sensation. Additionally, macrovascular complications were discussed, including the increased risk of myocardial infarction, stroke, and peripheral arterial disease."

**Concise (Preferred - 75% shorter)**:
> "Key Education: Disease understanding, micro/macrovascular complication risks, self-monitoring techniques (BG, BP), medication timing, diet basics, exercise safety, sick-day management. Key Red Flags: Hypoglycemia (shaking, confusion - treat with 15g carbs), severe hyperglycemia >300 (call clinic), chest pain/stroke symptoms (call 911)."

### Mental Health - Depression Example

**Verbose (Avoid)**:
> "The patient will participate in individual psychotherapy utilizing cognitive behavioral therapy techniques. Sessions will be scheduled on a weekly basis for a duration of 50 minutes each. The therapist will work with the patient to identify negative thought patterns, challenge cognitive distortions, develop behavioral activation strategies, and build coping skills for managing depressive symptoms."

**Concise (Preferred - 60% shorter)**:
> "CBT weekly × 16 (50 min), Focus: ID/challenge negative thoughts, behavioral activation, coping skill building. Goals: PHQ-9 <10, return to work, 3 effective stress management strategies."

### Rehabilitation - Post-Stroke Example

**Verbose (Avoid)**:
> "Expected outcomes include improvement in upper extremity function, with anticipated ability to perform self-care activities including bathing, dressing, and grooming with minimal assistance or independently. It is expected that the patient will demonstrate increased ambulation, progressing from wheelchair mobility to walking with a rolling walker under supervision, with a final goal of independent ambulation with a straight cane for up to 300 feet."

**Concise (Preferred - 70% shorter)**:
> "Expected Outcomes (8 weeks): Independent ADLs with assistive devices, ambulate 300+ ft with walker under supervision, use stairs with railing, safe discharge home. Timeline: Week 2 - supervised transfers; Week 4 - ambulate 150 ft; Week 8 - community ambulation, discharge ready."

### Perioperative - Laparoscopic Surgery Example

**Verbose (Avoid)**:
> "Postoperative pain management will utilize a multimodal analgesic regimen to minimize opioid consumption and reduce the risk of opioid-related adverse effects, including nausea, vomiting, constipation, and respiratory depression. The multimodal regimen will include scheduled acetaminophen 1000 mg every 6 hours, ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours as needed, with opioid analgesics reserved for breakthrough pain only."

**Concise (Preferred - 65% shorter)**:
> "Multimodal Analgesia: Acetaminophen 1000mg Q6H scheduled, Ibuprofen 600mg Q6H PRN, Opioids for breakthrough only. Goals: Pain <4/10, reduced opioid use, early mobilization."

### Key Principles for Concise Documentation

1.  **Appropriate use of abbreviations**: Q6H, PRN, ADLs, BP (Define on first use if uncommon)
2.  **Bullets over paragraphs**: Easier to scan, more actionable
3.  **Consolidate related information**: Group similar items together
4.  **Eliminate filler words**: "The patient will...", "It is expected..."
5.  **Focus on "What, When, Why"**: State the action, timing, and rationale in minimal words
6.  **Use tables for complex data**: e.g., medication lists, monitoring schedules
7.  **Prioritize critical information**: Safety red flags, urgent actions

## 1. General Medicine Treatment Plan

### Applicability
- Chronic conditions: Diabetes, Hypertension, Heart Failure, COPD, Asthma
- Common acute conditions requiring structured follow-up
- Primary care management of stable chronic diseases

### Key Assessment Components

**Baseline Status**:
- Vitals, BMI, functional status
- Disease-specific metrics (HbA1c, BP, Lipids, PFTs)
- Comorbidity assessment
- Medication reconciliation
- Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) screening

**Disease Severity Staging**:
- Use validated staging systems whenever possible
- Examples: CKD Stages 1-5, GOLD COPD Grades I-IV, NYHA Heart Failure Class I-IV, ADA Diabetes Complications
- Document severity to guide treatment intensity

### Treatment Goal Details

**Guideline-Based Targets**:
- HbA1c <7% for most ( <8% for elderly or limited life expectancy)
- BP <130/80 for most; <140/90 for elderly or low CV risk
- LDL <70 mg/dL if ASCVD present; <100 mg/dL for moderate risk
- Individualize targets based on patient factors

**Functional Goals**:
- Maintain independence in ADLs
- Return to work if applicable
- Participation in valued activities
- Quality of life improvement

### Medication Considerations

**Polypharmacy Management**:
- Consider deprescribing where possible (Beers Criteria for elderly)
- Reconcile medications at every visit
- Simplify regimens (once-daily dosing, combination pills)
- Address adherence barriers (cost, side effects, complexity)

**Drug-Disease Interactions**:
- Avoid NSAIDs in CKD, Heart Failure
- Caution with metformin if eGFR <30
- Beta-blockers contraindicated in severe COPD/Asthma (use cardio-selective if needed)

### Monitoring Plans by Condition

**Diabetes**:
- HbA1c every 3 months if not at goal; every 6 months if stable
- Annual: Dilated eye exam, foot exam, urine ACR, lipids
- Every visit: BP, weight, med adherence

**Hypertension**:
- Home Blood Pressure Monitoring (HBPM) - most accurate, average of multiple readings
- Office BP at every visit
- Labs (K+, Creatinine in BMP) 1-2 weeks after starting ACE/ARB, then annually

**Heart Failure**:
- Daily weights (report >2-3 lb gain in 2 days)
- BNP/NT-proBNP when clinical status changes
- Echocardiogram annually or if EF change suspected
- Medication titration every 2 weeks during optimization phase

### Primary Care Integration

**Preventive Care**:
- Include age-appropriate cancer screenings
- Vaccination schedule (Flu, Pneumococcal, Zoster, COVID)
- Lifestyle counseling (Tobacco, Alcohol, Diet, Exercise)

**Chronic Care Model**:
- Components: Self-management support, delivery system redesign, clinical info systems, decision support
- Team-based care: Involvement of nurses, pharmacists, dietitians, care coordinators

## 2. Rehabilitation Treatment Plan

### Applicability Settings
- Post-acute inpatient rehabilitation
- Outpatient PT/OT/SLP
- Home health therapy
- Skilled nursing facility rehab

### Key Assessment Components

**Functional Assessment (Use Validated Tools)**:
- **FIM** (Functional Independence Measure): 18 items, 7-point scale, max 126 - most widely used
- **Barthel Index**: 10 ADL items, 100-point scale - simpler than FIM
- **Berg Balance Scale**: 14 tasks, 56 points - fall risk (score <45 = high risk)
- **6-Minute Walk Test**: Distance walked in 6 min - cardiopulmonary endurance
- **Timed Up and Go (TUG)**: Time to stand, walk 3m, turn, return, sit - fall risk (>12 sec = high risk)
- **9-Hole Peg Test**: Fine motor speed for upper extremity
- **ROM**: Goniometric measurements for each joint
- **MMT** (Manual Muscle Testing): 0-5 scale (0=no contraction, 5=normal strength)

**ICF Framework Goals**:
- **Body Functions/Structures**: Impairments (ROM, strength, balance)
- **Activities**: Task performance (walking 150 ft, dressing independently)
- **Participation**: Life roles (return to work, community involvement)

### Rehabilitation Goal Details

**Goal Hierarchy**:
1.  **Impairment Goals**: Increase knee ROM 90→110°, improve MMT 3/5→4/5
2.  **Activity Goals**: Ambulate 300 ft with walker, independent bed-to-chair transfers
3.  **Participation Goals**: Return to work, resume hobby, live independently

**Assistance Levels** (Document current and target):
- I = Independent
- SV = Supervision (cues, no physical assist)
- CG = Contact Guard (hands on/near, no assist)
- Min A = Minimal Assist (patient does 75%+)
- Mod A = Moderate Assist (patient does 50-74%)
- Max A = Maximal Assist (patient does 25-49%)
- Total A = Total Assist (patient does <25%)

### Therapeutic Interventions

**Physical Therapy (PT)**:
- Therapeutic exercise dosing: Specify sets, reps, resistance, frequency
- Gait training: Distance, assistive device, level of supervision
- Balance training: Static, dynamic, perturbation-based
- Modalities: Heat, ice, TENS, e-stim - only as adjunct, not primary intervention

**Occupational Therapy (OT)**:
- ADL training: Use of assistive devices (reachers, sock aids, built-up utensils)
- UE strengthening: Functional tasks, fine motor activities
- Cognitive training: Memory strategies, attention training, executive function

**Speech-Language Pathology (SLP)**:
- Dysphagia: Diet consistency modification (IDDSI levels), swallowing strategies (chin tuck, double swallow)
- Aphasia therapy: Constraint-induced language therapy, semantic feature analysis
- Dysarthria: Articulation exercises, rate control, AAC

### Home Exercise Program (HEP)

**Elements**:
- Illustrated handouts with pictures/descriptions
- Specific dosing (e.g., "2 sets x 10 reps daily")
- Progression criteria
- Safety precautions
- Patient/caregiver demonstration of understanding

### DME and Environmental Modifications

**Common DME (Durable Medical Equipment)**:
- Ambulation: Walkers, canes, crutches (specify type, e.g., Front Wheeled Walker)
- Bathroom: Raised toilet seats, shower chairs, grab bars
- Dressing: Reachers, sock aids, long shoehorns, button hooks, elastic laces
- Mobility: Hospital beds, wheelchairs (if needed)

**Home Modifications**:
- Ramps for stairs
- Stairlifts for multi-level homes
- Removal of throw rugs (fall hazard)
- Improved lighting
- Rearranging furniture for accessibility

### Discharge Planning

**Discharge Criteria**:
- Functional plateau reached or goals met
- Safe discharge environment
- Patient/caregiver education completed
- DME obtained and home modifications finished
- Follow-up appointments scheduled

**Discharge Disposition**:
- Home with outpatient therapy
- Home with home health services
- Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF)
- Long-Term Acute Care Hospital (LTACH) if medically complex

## 3. Mental Health Treatment Plan

### Applicability
- Major Depressive Disorder, Dysthymia
- Anxiety Disorders (GAD, Panic, Social Anxiety, Specific Phobias)
- Bipolar Disorder
- Schizophrenia and Psychotic Disorders
- PTSD and Trauma-related disorders
- Eating Disorders
- Substance Use Disorders
- Personality Disorders

### Key Assessment Components

**Diagnostic Evaluation**:
- DSM-5 criteria met
- Symptom severity assessment (using validated scales)
- Functional impairment (work, relationships, self-care)
- Psychiatric history (prior episodes, treatments, hospitalizations)
- Substance use assessment (AUDIT, DAST)
- Trauma history
- Family psychiatric history

**Validated Assessment Tools**:
- **PHQ-9**: Depression severity (0-27, score ≥10 indicates moderate-severe)
- **GAD-7**: Anxiety severity (0-21, score ≥10 indicates moderate-severe)
- **MDQ** (Mood Disorder Questionnaire): Bipolar screening
- **PC-PTSD-5**: PTSD screening, followed by full PCL-5 if positive
- **AUDIT**: Alcohol use (0-40, ≥8 indicates hazardous drinking)
- **PHQ-15**: Somatic symptoms
- **WHODAS 2.0**: Functional disability

**Risk Assessment**:
- **Suicide Risk**: Use Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)
    - Ideation (passive, active, plan, intent)
    - Protective factors (reasons for living, social support)
    - Risk factors (prior attempts, impulsivity, access to means)
- **Violence/Homicide Risk**: History of violence, current ideation, access to weapons

### Treatment Goal Details

**Symptom Goals**:
- Reduction in standardized scale scores (e.g., PHQ-9 from 18→<10→<5 for remission)
- Specific symptom targets (7 hours of sleep, panic attacks from 3/week to 0)

**Functional Goals**:
- Return to work/school
- Resumption of social activities
- Improved interpersonal relationships
- Independence in self-care

**Recovery-Oriented Goals**:
- Personal meaning and purpose
- Hope and empowerment
- Social connectedness and community integration
- Independent living

### Evidence-Based Psychotherapy

**Depression**:
- **CBT**: 12-16 sessions, homework between sessions
- **Behavioral Activation**: Focus on increasing rewarding activities
- **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)**: 12-16 sessions, focus on relationships
- **Problem-Solving Therapy**: Short-term (6-8 sessions), structured approach

**Anxiety**:
- **CBT with Exposure**: Gold standard for anxiety disorders
- **Panic Control Treatment**: Interoceptive exposure, cognitive restructuring
- **Social Skills Training**: For social anxiety

**PTSD**:
- **Prolonged Exposure (PE)**: 8-15 sessions, imaginal and in-vivo exposure
- **Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)**: 12 sessions, challenging trauma-related cognitions
- **EMDR** (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing): Alternative, less evidence than PE/CPT

**Bipolar Disorder**:
- **Family-Focused Therapy**: Psychoeducation, communication, problem-solving
- **Interpersonal and Social Rhythm Therapy**: Stabilizing daily routines, sleep

**Borderline Personality Disorder**:
- **DBT** (Dialectical Behavior Therapy): 1-year program, individual + group + phone coaching
- Skills: Mindfulness, Distress Tolerance, Emotion Regulation, Interpersonal Effectiveness

### Psychopharmacology Details

**Antidepressants**:
- First-line: SSRIs (Sertraline, Escitalopram, Fluoxetine)
- Initial response in 2-4 weeks, full effect in 6-8 weeks
- Titrate after 2-4 weeks if partial response
- Switch if no response after full trial
- Augmentation strategies (2nd antidepressant, atypical antipsychotic, lithium) if partial response
- Continue for 6-12 months after remission (longer if recurrent)

**Antipsychotics**:
- 1st Generation (Typical): Haloperidol - high EPS risk, 2nd gen preferred
- 2nd Generation (Atypical): Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine, Aripiprazole, Lurasidone
- Monitoring: Metabolic syndrome (weight, glucose, lipids), EPS, prolactin, QTc

**Mood Stabilizers**:
- Lithium: Narrow therapeutic window, monitor levels (0.6-1.2 mEq/L), TSH, renal function
- Valproate: Monitor levels, LFTs, CBC (thrombocytopenia)
- Lamotrigine: Slow titration (risk of Stevens-Johnson Syndrome if too fast)

### Safety Planning

**Mandatory for all Mental Health plans**:
- Warning signs (thoughts, feelings, behaviors)
- Internal coping strategies
- Social contacts for support
- Professional contacts (therapist, psychiatrist, crisis line)
- Means restriction (removing firearms, limiting medications)
- Reasons for living

**Crisis Resources**:
- 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
- Crisis Text Line (Text HOME to 741741)
- Local mobile crisis teams
- Emergency Department

## 4. Chronic Disease Management Plan

### Multimorbidity Management

**Common Clusters**:
- Cardiovascular-Metabolic: Diabetes + Hypertension + Hyperlipidemia + Obesity
- Cardiopulmonary: Heart Failure + COPD
- Renal-Cardiovascular: CKD + Hypertension + Diabetes
- Psycho-Physiological: Depression + Chronic Pain + Chronic Disease

### Prioritization Strategies

**When multiple goals conflict**:
1.  **Life-threatening issues first**: Unstable angina, uncontrolled heart failure
2.  **High-impact, actionable conditions**: Diabetes with HbA1c 10% (significant room for improvement)
3.  **Synergistic treatments**: Medications that work for multiple conditions (e.g., SGLT2i for DM + HF + CKD)
4.  **Patient priorities**: What matters most to the patient

### Medication Optimization for Multimorbidity

**Synergistic Meds** (Double/Triple Wins):
- **SGLT2 Inhibitors**: Diabetes + Heart Failure + CKD
- **ACE Inhibitors/ARBs**: HTN + Diabetes (renal protection) + Heart Failure
- **Beta-blockers**: HTN + Heart Failure + CAD
- **Statins**: Hyperlipidemia + ASCVD prevention + Diabetes
- **GLP-1 Agonists**: Diabetes + Weight loss + CV benefit

**Deprescribing**:
- Identify medications with limited benefit (e.g., tight glycemic control in limited life expectancy)
- Stop medications where risks outweigh benefits
- Simplify regimens (reduce pill burden)

### Care Coordination

**Team-Based Care**:
- PCP as the coordinator
- Specialists for co-management (Cardiology for HF, Endocrine for DM)
- Care coordinators to assist with scheduling, education, and identifying barriers
- Pharmacists for med review and optimization
- Dietitians for Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
- Social workers for social needs

**Communication**:
- Use shared EHR whenever possible
- Treatment plan visible to all team members
- Medication reconciliation after specialist visits
- Regular team huddles or e-consults

### Population Health Integration

**Registry Management**:
- Identify patients due for care (HbA1c testing, diabetic eye exams)
- Outreach for overdue preventive care
- Risk stratification (high utilizers, complex patients)

**Transitional Care Management**:
- Follow-up within 7 days of hospital discharge
- Post-discharge medication reconciliation
- Review of red flags
- Escalation plan for worsening symptoms

## 5. Perioperative Care Plan

### Preoperative Risk Assessment

**Cardiac Risk** (Revised Cardiac Risk Index - RCRI):
- High-risk surgery, Ischemic heart disease, Heart failure, CVD, Insulin-dependent DM, Creatinine >2
- 0 points = <1% risk, 1 point = 1%, 2 points = 2.4%, ≥3 points = 5.4% risk of cardiac events

**If high risk**: Consider further testing (stress test, echo), cardiology consult, perioperative beta-blockers.

**Pulmonary Risk** (ARISCAT Score):
- Age, SpO2, recent respiratory infection, pre-op anemia, surgical incision, duration of surgery, emergency
- Higher risk: Smoking cessation, incentive spirometry use, early mobilization

**VTE Risk** (Caprini Score):
- Age, type of surgery, mobility, prior VTE, obesity, cancer
- Stratification guides prophylaxis (none, mechanical, pharmacologic, or both)

### Preoperative Optimization

**Diabetes**:
- Target HbA1c <8% for elective surgery (postpone if >9%)
- Stop metformin 24-48h pre-op (lactic acidosis risk)
- Stop SGLT2i 3-4 days pre-op (DKA risk)
- Insulin: Reduce long-acting by 20-25% day of surgery, hold short-acting

**Hypertension**:
- Continue most medications through surgery
- Hold ACE/ARB morning of surgery (avoid intraoperative hypotension)
- Continue beta-blockers (avoid withdrawal)

**Anticoagulation**:
- Warfarin: Hold 5 days prior, bridge with LMWH if high VTE risk
- DOACs: Hold 24-48h based on renal function and bleeding risk
- Antiplatelets: Continue aspirin for most, hold P2Y12 inhibitors (clopidogrel) 5-7 days prior if high bleeding risk

**Anemia**:
- Optimize iron stores pre-op (IV iron if time is short)
- Avoid transfusion induction whenever possible (restrictive strategy)

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS)

**Preoperative**:
- Patient education, expectation setting
- No prolonged fasting (clear liquids up to 2h pre-op)
- Carbohydrate loading (reduces insulin resistance)
- No routine pre-medication

**Intraoperative**:
- Multimodal analgesia (minimize opioids)
- Goal-directed fluid therapy (avoid fluid overload)
- Normothermia management (prevent hypothermia)
- PONV prophylaxis

**Postoperative**:
- Early mobilization (out of bed day of surgery)
- Early oral nutrition (resume diet POD 0-1)
- Multimodal analgesia (Acetaminophen, NSAIDs, regional blocks)
- Early removal of tubes (foley, NG, surgical drains)
- DVT prophylaxis

### Postoperative Milestones

**Day of Surgery (POD 0)**:
- Out of bed to chair 4-6h post-op
- Clear liquids as tolerated
- Pain well-controlled on multimodal regimen

**Post-op Day 1 (POD 1)**:
- Ambulate in hallway
- Regular diet
- Foley catheter removed
- Transition to oral pain meds

**Post-op Day 2-3** (Typical discharge for many surgeries):
- Ambulate 150+ feet
- Adequate oral intake
- Pain well-controlled on oral meds
- No complications requiring hospitalization

### Discharge Readiness

**Criteria**:
- Pain adequately controlled with oral medications
- Tolerating regular diet
- Mobile (ambulating, transferring)
- Voiding spontaneously
- Stable vital signs
- No active complications
- Safe discharge plan (home support, DME arranged)

## 6. Pain Management Plan

### Pain Assessment

**Comprehensive Pain Evaluation**:
- Location, radiation
- Quality (sharp, dull, burning, aching, electric)
- Intensity (0-10 NRS)
- Temporal pattern (constant, intermittent, episodic)
- Aggravating/alleviating factors
- Functional impact (BPI interference items)
- Prior treatments and response

**Pain Classification**:
- **Nociceptive**: Somatic (musculoskeletal) or Visceral (organs)
- **Neuropathic**: Nerve damage/dysfunction (burning, electric, numbness/tingling)
- **Nociplastic**: Central sensitization, fibromyalgia
- **Mixed**: Combination of types

### Multimodal Analgesia Principles

**Goal**: Use multiple mechanisms to produce additive/synergistic pain relief with opioid-sparing effects.

**Components**:
1.  Non-opioid analgesics (Acetaminophen, NSAIDs)
2.  Adjuvant analgesics (Gabapentinoids, SNRIs, TCAs for neuropathic)
3.  Topical agents (Lidocaine patches, Diclofenac gel, Capsaicin)
4.  Interventional treatments (Injections, nerve blocks, RFA, SCS)
5.  Physical therapy (PT, exercise, TENS)
6.  Psychological therapy (CBT-CP, mindfulness, biofeedback)
7.  Complementary therapies (Acupuncture, massage, yoga)
8.  Opioids (only if others insufficient) - lowest dose, frequent reassessment

### Neuropathic Pain Specific Treatment

**First-line**:
- Gabapentin 300mg titrated to 1800-3600mg/day in TID dosing
- Pregabalin 75mg BID titrated to 150-300mg BID
- Duloxetine 60mg daily (also for fibromyalgia, chronic MSK pain)
- TCAs (Amitriptyline, Nortriptyline) 10-75mg QHS - 2nd line due to side effects

**Topical**:
- Lidocaine patch 5% (for localized neuropathic pain)
- Capsaicin 8% patch (high concentration, office-based)

**Refractory**:
- Tramadol (dual mechanism - opioid + SNRI)
- Opioids (if severe and function-limiting despite above)

### Opioid Prescribing (CDC Guidelines)

**Before Starting**:
- Non-opioid multimodal therapy tried and insufficient
- Establish functional goals (not just pain scores)
- Discuss and document risks vs. benefits
- Opioid risk assessment (ORT, SOAPP)
- Informed consent discussion
- Treatment agreement signed
- Check PDMP
- Baseline UDS (Urine Drug Screen)

**During Opioid Therapy**:
- Start with low dose (<50 MME/day), short-acting
- Reassess frequently (every 1-3 months)
- Expect functional improvement (not just pain scores)
- UDS every 3-6 months (check adherence and illicit substances)
- Check PDMP at every prescription or at least every 3 months
- Co-prescribe Naloxone
- Avoid concurrent benzodiazepines
- Re-evaluate if dose approaches 50 MME; avoid >90 MME if possible

**Tapering/Discontinuation**:
- If functional goals not met
- Serious adverse effects occur
- Aberrant behaviors present
- Patient request
- Taper slowly: 10-25% dose reduction per week to month (faster if safety concerns)

### Interventional Pain Procedures

**Indications and Evidence**:
- **Epidural Steroid Injections**: Radicular pain from disc herniation/stenosis - short-term benefit
- **Facet Injections**: Diagnostic (if >50% relief, proceed to RFA)
- **Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)**: Provides 6-12 months of relief for facet-mediated pain
- **Spinal Cord Stimulation (SCS)**: Refractory neuropathic pain (FBSS, CRPS) - 50-60% success rate
- **Intrathecal Pumps**: Severe refractory pain, cancer pain - delivers meds to CSF

**Procedure Note Elements**:
- Indication, prior conservative treatments tried
- Expected benefit and duration
- Risks discussed
- Number of injections/procedures allowed per year

### Emphasizing Functional Goals

**Shift from Pain Scores to Function**:
- "Reduce pain to 3/10" is less meaningful than "Walk 1 mile, return to work, play with grandkids"
- BPI interference scores track functional impact
- SMART functional goals (see Goal Setting reference)

### Psychological Integration

**CBT for Chronic Pain (CBT-CP)**:
- Pain education and reconceptualization (pain ≠ damage)
- Cognitive restructuring (challenging catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking)
- Activity pacing and graded exposure (increasing activity without flares)
- Relaxation techniques
- Acceptance and mindfulness

**Chronic Pain Pearl**: Psychological factors (depression, anxiety, catastrophizing) perpetuate pain; they must be addressed.

## Universal Considerations for All Treatment Plans

### Cultural Competence
- Inquire about cultural health beliefs and practices
- Use interpreter services when language barriers exist
- Respect religious/spiritual practices in treatment
- Adapt interventions to cultural context where possible

### Health Literacy
- Assess understanding (Teach-back method)
- Use plain language, avoid medical jargon
- Provide visual aids and written materials at 5th-6th grade reading level
- Confirm patient can perform the plan (demonstrate inhaler use, insulin injection, etc.)

### Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)
- Screen for food insecurity, housing instability, transportation barriers
- Connect with community resources (SNAP, Medicaid, patient assistance programs)
- Address barriers in the treatment plan (e.g., cost of meds → generic alternatives, patient assistance)

### Advance Care Planning
- Appropriate for serious illness, elderly, or those with functional decline
- Goals of care discussion
- Designation of healthcare proxy
- Completion of Advance Directive
- Preferences regarding CPR, intubation, dialysis, etc.
