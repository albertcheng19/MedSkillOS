# Budget Justification Template

## Overview

A Budget Justification provides a detailed explanation for every item in the budget, demonstrating that these costs are necessary, reasonable, and directly related to the proposed research. The justification should be sufficiently detailed to allow reviewers to understand and evaluate the reasonableness of the costs.

**Core Principles**:
- Justify **every single** expenditure based on the research plan
- Clearly explain the calculation process
- Explain why these costs are essential for the proposed work
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness where possible
- Follow the specific formatting and requirements of the funding agency

*Calculation*: 
- Year 1: $120,000 × 0.2083 = $25,000
- Years 2-5: 3% annual increase

### Postdoctoral Scholars

**Postdoctoral Researcher (1.0 FTE)**

**Justification**:
A full-time postdoctoral researcher is essential for conducting [which experiments/aims]. The postdoc will be responsible for [specific experimental/technical activities], data analysis, and mentoring graduate students. Specific responsibilities include: [list 4-6 key responsibilities related to specific aims]. We will recruit a candidate with expertise in [required skills/background].

**Calculation**:
- Year 1: NIH NRSA Stipend Level Year 0-2 ($54,840) + Fringe Benefits (26%) = $69,099
- Years 2-3: Adjusted based on postdoc seniority levels
- Years 4-5: Senior postdoc rates

**Example**:
*Postdoctoral Researcher (1.0 FTE)*

*Justification*: A full-time postdoc is essential for executing the cellular and molecular experiments in Aims 2-3. The postdoc will: (1) generate and characterize patient-derived iPSC lines, (2) differentiate iPSCs into beta cells, hepatocytes, and adipocytes, (3) perform functional assays (insulin secretion, glucose uptake, cytokine profiling), (4) conduct proteomics sample preparation and analysis, (5) integrate cellular data with clinical outcomes, and (6) mentor graduate students in cell culture techniques. We will recruit a candidate with expertise in stem cell biology and diabetes research. The postdoc will have opportunities for career development through the institution's K99/R00 preparation program.

*Calculation*:
- Year 1: $54,840 (NIH Year 0) + $14,258 (26% fringe) = $69,098
- Year 2: $56,784 (NIH Year 1) + $14,764 = $71,548
- Year 3: $59,292 (NIH Year 2) + $15,416 = $74,708

## Equipment (Unit cost of $5,000 or more)

**[Equipment Name and Model]**

**Cost**: $[Amount]

**Justification**:
This equipment is essential for [which aims/experiments]. Our institution currently lacks [this specific capability]. The [Equipment] will be used for [specific application in the project]. [Expected usage: hours/week or % of time dedicated to this project]. This equipment will support [how many students/researchers] and will continue to be useful for future projects in [field].

**Example**:
*BD FACSAria III Cell Sorter with 4-laser configuration*

*Cost*: $425,000

*Justification*: A high-speed cell sorter is critical for Aim 2 experiments, which require isolating specific cell populations from heterogeneous patient-derived cultures (beta cells, hepatocytes, adipocytes) for subsequent proteomics and functional analysis. Our institution's current analyzers have a 6-month wait time and lack the 4-laser capability required for our 8-color panels. This sorter will be used 15 hours per week for this project and will support 3 graduate students and 1 postdoc. The equipment will be housed in the Biology Department Core Facility and will be open to 15 other labs after this project, ensuring long-term institutional value. The equipment cost includes installation, training, and a 5-year service contract.

## Tips for Writing a High-Quality Budget Justification

✅ **Do**:
- Link every cost directly to specific research aims and activities
- Provide detailed calculations showing how you arrived at the numbers
- Explain why the amount is necessary and reasonable
- Use institutional or national standard rates
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness where possible
- Include escalation (inflation) in subsequent years
- Clearly specify equipment models, conference names, etc.

❌ **Don't**:
- Use vague language (e.g., "miscellaneous supplies")
- Forget to provide a justification for any item in the budget
- Set aside excessive budget for unforeseen expenditures (Contingency)
- Include costs unrelated to the proposed work
- Underestimate costs (this creates problems if funded)
- Forget agency-specific cost limits (salary caps, F&A exclusions)

## Agency-Specific Notes

**NIH**: 
- Salary caps apply (approx. $221,900 in 2024)
- Modular budgets (direct costs ≤$250K) require less detail
- Participant support costs are excluded from F&A

**NSF**:
- No salary cap
- Faculty on 9-month appointments are typically limited to 2 summer months of salary
- Cost sharing is not required (except for specific programs)

**DOE**:
- Typically requires detailed budgets by quarter
- Cost sharing may be required
- Equipment often requires special justification

**DARPA**:
- Detailed costs listed by phase and task
- Supporting cost data usually required
- Rate approval may be required (DCAA audit for industry)
