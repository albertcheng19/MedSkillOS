# Grant Proposal Requirements

Comprehensive requirements and formatting guidelines for major federal and private foundation grant programs.

**Last Updated**: 2024

---

## NSF (National Science Foundation)

### Overview

**Agency**: National Science Foundation  
**Typical Funding**: $100,000 to $500,000 per year, for 3-5 years  
**Success Rate**: 20-25% (varies by program)  
**Review Criteria**: Intellectual Merit + Broader Impacts (weighted equally)

---

### NSF Standard Grant Proposal

**Page Limits (Reference: NSF PAPPG - Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide)**:

| Component | Page Limit | Font | Line Spacing |
|-----------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Project Summary** | 1 page | Any readable font, 10pt+ | Any |
| **Project Description** | 15 pages | Times New Roman 11pt or similar | Single-spaced |
| **References Cited** | No limit | Times New Roman 11pt | Single-spaced |
| **Biographical Sketch** | 3 pages per person | Times New Roman 11pt | Single-spaced |
| **Budget Justification** | 3-5 pages | Any readable font | Any |
| **Current & Pending Support** | No limit | Times New Roman 11pt | Single-spaced |
| **Facilities, Equipment** | 2 pages | Any readable font | Any |
| **Data Management Plan** | 2 pages | Any readable font | Any |

**Margins**: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides (strictly enforced)

---

### NSF Project Summary (1 page)

**Required Sections** (must be clearly labeled):

1. **Overview** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Concise description of research activities
   - Goals and methods

2. **Intellectual Merit** (1 paragraph)
   - How the project advances knowledge
   - Innovation and transformative potential
   - Qualifications of the research team

3. **Broader Impacts** (1 paragraph)
   - Benefits to society
   - Broadening participation
   - Dissemination and outreach

**Format**: Can be full-page text or divided into sections  
**Audience**: Non-specialists (the broader scientific community)

**Template**: `assets/grants/nsf_project_summary.tex`

---

### NSF Project Description (15 pages)

**Typical Structure**:

1. **Introduction/Background** (2-3 pages)
   - Current state of knowledge
   - Research gaps
   - Preliminary work/feasibility
   - Team qualifications

2. **Research Plan** (8-10 pages)
   - Objectives and hypotheses
   - Methods and approaches
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Expected outcomes

3. **Broader Impacts** (1-2 pages)
   - Educational activities
   - Broadening participation (underrepresented groups)
   - Dissemination (publications, conferences, public outreach)
   - Societal benefits

4. **Results from Prior NSF Support** (1 page, if applicable)
   - Required if the PI has received NSF funding in the last 5 years
   - Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts of prior work
   - Publications resulting from prior NSF grants

**Core Requirements**:
- Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts should be integrated throughout
- Figures and tables are allowed (count toward page limit)
- Cite references (use the "References Cited" section)

**Template**: `assets/grants/nsf_proposal_template.tex`

---

### NSF Biographical Sketch (3 pages)

**Required Sections**:
1. **Professional Preparation**: Institutions, degrees, fields
2. **Appointments**: Current and previous positions
3. **Products**: Up to 5 most relevant products, up to 5 other significant products
   - Can include publications, datasets, software, patents
4. **Synergistic Activities**: Up to 5 examples of impact beyond research

**Format**:
- Must use NSF templates (SciENcv or NSF-approved format)
- No longer uses the title "Publications"; changed to "Products"

---

### NSF Broader Impacts

**NSF-Recognized Categories** (must demonstrate at least one):
1. **Advance discovery while promoting teaching/learning**
2. **Broaden participation of underrepresented groups**
3. **Broad dissemination** to enhance scientific/technological understanding
4. **Benefit to society** (economy, health, environment, national security)
5. **Develop the scientific workforce** and infrastructure

**Best Practices**:
- Be specific with measurable outcomes
- Explain how these activities will be evaluated
- Integrate organically with the research (do not treat as an "add-on")
- Budget for Broader Impacts activities

**Examples**:
- K-12 outreach programs
- Curriculum development
- Training underrepresented students
- Public science communication
- Open-source software development

---

### NSF Budget

**Typical Categories**:
- **Senior Personnel**: PI, co-PIs (percentage of effort, salary)
- **Other Personnel**: Postdocs, graduate students, undergraduates
- **Fringe Benefits**: Institutional rates
- **Equipment**: Items with a unit cost >$5,000
- **Travel**: Domestic and international
- **Participant Support**: Workshops, conferences (separate category)
- **Other Direct Costs**: Materials, publication costs, subawards
- **Indirect Costs**: Institutional F&A rates

**Budget Justification**: Explain the necessity of each expenditure

---

### NSF Data Management Plan (2 pages)

**Required Content**:
- Types of data produced
- Data formats and metadata standards
- Policies for access and sharing
- Policies for re-use and redistribution
- Plans for archiving and preservation

**Approved Methods**:
- Deposit in domain-specific repositories
- Institutional repositories
- Data available upon request (must justify restrictions)

---

### NSF Review Process

**Review Criteria** (weighted equally):

1. **Intellectual Merit**:
   - What is the potential to advance knowledge?
   - How well-conceived and organized is the idea?
   - How qualified are the PI and the team?
   - Are the resources adequate?

2. **Broader Impacts**:
   - What are the potential benefits to society?
   - Is the plan for achieving broader impacts appropriate?

**Panel Review**: Proposals are reviewed by a panel of experts  
**Timeline**: Usually takes 6 months from the deadline to a funding decision

## NIH (National Institutes of Health)

### Overview

**Agency**: National Institutes of Health  
**Funding Mechanisms**: 
- **R01**: Research Project Grant (most common)
- **R21**: Exploratory/Developmental Research Grant
- **K Awards**: Career Development Awards
**Success Rate**: 10-20% (varies by institute and mechanism)

---

### NIH R01 Research Grant

**Page Limits** (Research Strategy):

| Component | Page Limit | Font | Line Spacing |
|-----------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Specific Aims** | 1 page | Arial min 11pt | Any |
| **Research Strategy** | 12 pages | Arial min 11pt | Min 0.5" margins |
| - Significance | Included in 12 pages | | |
| - Innovation | Included in 12 pages | | |
| - Approach | Included in 12 pages | | |
| **Bibliography** | No limit | Arial 11pt | |
| **Biographical Sketch** | 5 pages per person | Arial 11pt | |

**Margins**: Minimum 0.5 inches on all sides  
**Paper Size**: Letter (8.5 × 11 inches)

---

### NIH Specific Aims Page (1 page)

**The most critical section**

**Structure** (Recommended):

1. **Opening Paragraph** (2-3 sentences)
   - The Hook: Severity of the problem
   - The Gap: What is currently unknown

2. **Long-term Goal** (1 sentence)
   - Grand research vision

3. **Objective** (1-2 sentences)
   - What this proposal will accomplish
   - Central hypothesis

4. **Rationale** (2-3 sentences)
   - Why you expect to succeed
   - Preliminary data supporting the hypothesis

5. **Specific Aims** (Usually 3 aims)
   - **Aim 1**: [Title]. [1-2 sentence description. Working hypothesis. Expected outcome.]
   - **Aim 2**: [Title]. [1-2 sentence description. Working hypothesis. Expected outcome.]
   - **Aim 3**: [Title]. [1-2 sentence description. Working hypothesis. Expected outcome.]

6. **Closing Paragraph** (2-3 sentences)
   - Impact and Significance
   - Innovation
   - Future directions

**Best Practices**:
- Clear and engaging narrative
- Explicitly state hypotheses
- Explain expected outcomes
- Demonstrate innovation and impact

**Template**: `assets/grants/nih_specific_aims.tex`

---

### NIH Research Strategy (12 pages)

**Required Sections**:

#### 1. Significance (Usually 2-3 pages)
- **Importance**: Critical barriers to progress
- **Knowledge Gap**: Unknown territory
- **Impact**: How the project advances the field
- **Rigor**: Scientific premise/prior work
- **Citations**: Cite key literature

#### 2. Innovation (Usually 1-2 pages)
- **Novelty**: New concepts, approaches, or methods
- **Challenge Paradigms**: Shifting ways of thinking
- **Improved/New Methodology**: Technical innovation
- **Novel Application**: Using existing tools in new ways

#### 3. Approach (Usually 7-9 pages)
**For each Aim**:
- **Rationale**: Why this aim exists
- **Experimental Design**: Detailed methods
- **Expected Outcomes**: Significance of results
- **Potential Problems & Alternative Strategies**: Contingency plans
- **Rigor & Reproducibility**: Controls, replication, statistics
- **Timeline**: Completion time for each aim

**Additional Content for Approach**:
- Preliminary Data (crucial for R01)
- Power analyses for sample sizes
- Statistical analysis plan
- Rigor of prior research cited

---

### NIH Biographical Sketch (5 pages)

**Sections** (NIH Format):
1. **Personal Statement** (4 sentences explaining why you are suited for this project)
2. **Positions, Honors, and Scientific Appointments**
3. **Contributions to Science** (Up to 5 contributions, max 4 publications each)
4. **Research Support** (Current and completed grants, check for overlap)

**Format**: Must use NIH templates (fillable PDF or format pages)

---

### NIH Review Criteria

**Scoring Criteria** (1-9 scale, 1 is best):
1. **Significance**: Importance, impact
2. **Investigator(s)**: Qualifications, track record
3. **Innovation**: Novel concepts, methods
4. **Approach**: Feasibility, rigor, design
5. **Environment**: Institutional support, resources

**Other Considerations** (Not scored but noted):
- Vertebrate Animals
- Biohazards
- Protection of Human Subjects
- Inclusion of Women, Minorities, and Children
- Budget justification

**Overall Impact Score**: 1-9 (synthesizing all criteria)

---

### NIH R21 (Exploratory Grant)

**Key Differences from R01**:
- **Research Strategy**: 6 pages (vs. 12 for R01)
- **Duration**: Up to 2 years
- **Budget**: $275,000 total direct costs over 2 years
- **Preliminary Data**: Not required (due to exploratory nature)
- **Purpose**: High-risk, high-reward projects; new directions

**Choosing between R21 and R01**:
- R21: Early stage, limited preliminary data, high risk
- R01: Established research direction, supported by strong preliminary data

---

### NIH K Awards (Career Development)

**Mechanisms**:
- **K01**: Mentored Research Scientist Development Award
- **K08**: Mentored Clinical Scientist Research Career Development Award
- **K23**: Mentored Patient-Oriented Research Career Development Award
- **K99/R00**: Pathway to Independence Award (Postdoc to Faculty transition)

**Core Components**:
- **Career Development Plan**: Training goals, timeline
- **Research Plan**: 6-12 pages (depending on mechanism)
- **Mentors**: Letters of support, mentoring plan
- **Institutional Commitment**: Environment, resources
- **Protected Time**: Usually requires 75% research effort

---

### NIH Budget

**Modular vs. Detailed**:
- **Modular**: Direct costs ≤$250,000 per year (in increments of $25,000)
- **Detailed**: Direct costs >$250,000 per year

**Modular Budget**: Only requires budget justifications for personnel, consortium, and equipment >$25,000.

**Budget Periods**: Prepared year-by-year (R01 is typically 5 years)

## DOE (Department of Energy)

### Overview

**Agency**: U.S. Department of Energy  
**Offices**:
- **Office of Science**: Basic research (BES, BER, ASCR, NP, HEP, FES)
- **ARPA-E**: High-risk, high-reward energy technologies
- **EERE**: Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy

**Typical Funding**: $200,000 to $1,000,000 per year, for 3 years  
**Success Rate**: 10-30% (varies by program)

---

### DOE Office of Science Proposal

**Page Limits** (Typical, varies by specific FOA):

| Component | Page Limit | Format |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Project Narrative** | 10-20 pages | Times 11pt, 1" margins |
| **References** | No limit | |
| **Budget Justification** | 3-5 pages | |
| **Biographical Sketch** | 2-3 pages per person | |
| **Current & Pending Support** | No limit | |
| **Facilities & Resources** | No limit | |
| **Data Management Plan** | 2 pages | |

---

### DOE Project Narrative Structure

**Typical Sections**:

1. **Background and Significance** (2-3 pages)
   - Energy relevance
   - Current state of knowledge
   - Research needs

2. **Preliminary Work** (1-2 pages)
   - Team qualifications
   - Relevant preliminary results

3. **Research Plan** (10-15 pages)
   - **Objectives**: Clear metrics
   - **Technical Approach**: Detailed methods
   - **Milestones and Deliverables**: Specific, measurable
   - **Timeline**: Often uses Gantt charts
   - **Team and Management**: Roles, collaboration

4. **Broader Impacts** (1-2 pages)
   - Workforce development
   - Technology transfer potential
   - Publications and dissemination

---

### DOE Specific Requirements

**Energy Relevance**: Must explicitly align with the DOE mission
- Basic Science: Fundamental understanding for energy applications
- Applied Research: Efficiency, renewables, grid, storage

**Technology Readiness Levels (TRLs)**: Often required to be noted
- **TRL 1-3**: Basic research, proof of concept
- **TRL 4-6**: Component/subsystem validation
- **TRL 7-9**: System demonstration, deployment

**National Laboratory Collaboration**: Collaboration is encouraged
- Include Lab scientists as co-PIs or collaborators
- Provide letters of collaboration from Labs

**Cost Sharing**: Sometimes required (check FOA)
- Can be in-kind (equipment, time)
- Must be documented

---

### DOE Budget Considerations

**Allowable Costs**:
- Personnel (salaries, benefits)
- Equipment
- Travel (especially to DOE National Labs)
- Materials and supplies
- Subcontracts
- Indirect costs (negotiated F&A rates)

**Unallowable Costs**:
- Building construction
- Entertainment expenses
- Certain indirect costs (depending on institution type)

## DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)

### Overview

**Agency**: Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DoD)  
**Mission**: High-risk, high-reward research for national security  
**Typical Funding**: $500,000 to $5,000,000 per year, for 2-4 years  
**Success Rate**: 5-15% (extremely competitive)

---

### DARPA BAA (Broad Agency Announcement) Response

**Page Limits** (Typical, varies by specific BAA):

| Component | Page Limit | Format |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Technical & Management Proposal** | 20-25 pages | Times 12pt, 1" margins |
| **Cost Proposal** | Separate volume | |

---

### DARPA Technical Proposal Structure

**Key Sections**:

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Vision and impact
   - Technical approach overview
   - Team qualifications

2. **Heilmeier Catechism** (1-2 pages)
   DARPA requires answers to these questions:
   - **What are you trying to do?** Articulate goals without jargon
   - **How is it done today, and what are the limits?** Status quo and shortcomings
   - **What is new in your approach?** Innovation
   - **Who cares?** Impact if successful
   - **If you are successful, what difference will it make?** Transformative nature
   - **What are the risks?** Technical risks and mitigation
   - **How much will it cost?** Budget overview
   - **How long will it take?** Timeline
   - **What are the "mid-term" and "final exams"?** Milestones for success

3. **Technical Approach** (10-15 pages)
   - Detailed technical plan
   - Task breakdown
   - Risk mitigation
   - Innovation justification

4. **Management Plan** (2-3 pages)
   - Team organization
   - Key personnel roles
   - Collaboration methods
   - Milestone schedule (Gantt chart)

5. **Capabilities and Experience** (2-3 pages)
   - Team qualifications
   - Relevant facilities and equipment
   - Past similar programs

6. **Transition Plan** (1-2 pages)
   - Path to DoD transition
   - Identified end-users
   - Technology transfer methods

---

### DARPA Specific Considerations

**Engagement with Program Managers (PMs)**:
- **Highly recommended** to contact the PM before submission
- Discuss if the idea aligns with program goals
- PMs can provide feedback on the approach

**Transformative Impact**:
- Must demonstrate "game-changing" potential
- Not incremental improvements

**Technical Risk**:
- High-risk approaches are accepted (even encouraged)
- Must demonstrate risk mitigation strategies

**National Security Relevance**:
- Clear link to defense applications
- Dual-use is often valuable

**Success Metrics**:
- Quantifiable milestones
- "Go/no-go" decision points

---

### DARPA Budget

**Full Cost Accounting**: Requires detailed breakdown
- **Labor**: Hourly rates, hours per task
- **Materials**: Itemized list
- **Equipment**: Purchase justification
- **Travel**: Specific trips and purposes
- **Subcontracts**: Detailed sub-budgets
- **Indirect Costs**: Negotiated rates

**Cost Realism**: Budget must be realistic for the proposed work

## Private Foundations

### Gates Foundation

**Focus Areas**: Global health, poverty alleviation, education  
**Typical Funding**: Varies widely ($100,000 to $10M+)

**Proposal Requirements**:
- **Letter of Inquiry (LOI)** (2-3 pages): Initial screening
- **Full Proposal** (if invited): 10-15 pages
- **Theory of Change**: How interventions lead to impact
- **Monitoring & Evaluation**: Metrics, data collection

**Core Emphasis**:
- Scalability and sustainability
- Impact in low-resource settings
- Partnerships with local organizations
- Data-driven decision making

---

### Wellcome Trust

**Focus Areas**: Biomedical research, global health  
**Geography**: UK and international  
**Typical Funding**: £100,000 to £5,000,000

**Proposal Formats** (varies by scheme):
- **Investigator Awards**: Track record and research vision
- **Project Grants**: Specific research projects
- **Career Development**: For early/mid-career researchers

**Requirements**:
- Research plan
- Track record
- Justification for value for money
- Patient and public involvement

**Focus Areas**: Biomedical research, early-career scientists

**Selection**:

- Record of innovation
- Research vision for the next 5-7 years
- Potential for scientific leadership

## General Best Practices

### Writing Effective Proposals

1. **Start Early**: At least 2-3 months in advance
2. **Read the Announcement Carefully**: Follow every requirement strictly
3. **Know Your Reviewers**: Write for an expert audience
4. **Tell a Story**: Have a logical and engaging narrative
5. **Be Specific**: Concrete goals, methods, and outcomes
6. **Demonstrate Feasibility**: Preliminary data, expertise
7. **Address Weaknesses**: Acknowledge and mitigate risks

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Vague Goals**: "Understand X" → "Determine if X causes Y"
2. **Lack of Innovation**: Incremental improvements vs. transformative breakthroughs
3. **Poor Broader Impacts** (NSF): Generic statements, not integrated with research
4. **Weak Specific Aims** (NIH): This is the most critical page!
5. **Missing Preliminary Data**: Fails to prove feasibility
6. **Unrealistic Timeline**: Be honest about achievable progress
7. **Formatting Violations**: Can lead to immediate rejection
8. **Typos and Errors**: Suggests a lack of rigor

### Proposal Development Timeline

**3 Months Before Deadline**:
- Identify opportunity
- Assemble team
- Draft outline of goals

**2 Months Before Deadline**:
- Draft Specific Aims
- Preliminary budget
- Contact Program Officer (if allowed)

**1 Month Before Deadline**:
- Complete first full draft
- Internal review
- Revise based on feedback

**2 Weeks Before Deadline**:
- Final revisions
- Careful proofreading
- Assemble all documents

**1 Week Before Deadline**:
- Institutional review/approval
- Finalize budget
- Upload to submission systems

**2 Days Before Deadline**:
- Final check
- Submit (don't wait until the last minute!)

## Summary

**Key Takeaways**:

1. **Know the Agency**: Different missions, different emphases
2. **Follow the Rules**: Strictly adhere to page limits, fonts, and margins
3. **Tell a Compelling Story**: Clear problem, innovative solution, feasible plan
4. **Demonstrate Impact**: Intellectual merit (NSF/NIH) or mission relevance (DOE/DARPA)
5. **Prove Feasibility**: Preliminary data, team expertise, resource support
6. **Justify the Budget**: Provide rationale for all costs
7. **Proofread Carefully**: Typos damage credibility
8. **Submit Early**: Technical glitches can happen at any time

**Remember**: Grant writing is a skill that takes time to hone. Seek feedback, revise constantly, and be persistent!
