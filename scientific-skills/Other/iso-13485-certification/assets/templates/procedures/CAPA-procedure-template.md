## 1. Purpose

This procedure establishes requirements for:

- Taking corrective actions to eliminate the causes of nonconformities and prevent recurrence
- Taking preventive actions to eliminate the causes of potential nonconformities and prevent occurrence
- Systematic investigation and analysis of problems and risks
- Implementing and verifying effective actions
- Continual improvement of the Quality Management System (QMS)

This procedure ensures compliance with ISO 13485:2016 Clause 8.5.2 (Corrective Action) and Clause 8.5.3 (Preventive Action).

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All nonconformities identified through any means (internal audits, customer complaints, process monitoring, product inspection, etc.)
- Potential nonconformities identified through risk analysis, trend analysis, and proactive reviews
- All products, processes, and Quality Management System (QMS) elements
- All departments and personnel within [Company Name]

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CAPA** | Corrective and Preventive Action |
| **Corrective Action** | Action taken to eliminate the cause of a detected nonconformity or other undesirable situation to prevent recurrence |
| **Preventive Action** | Action taken to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity or other potential undesirable situation to prevent occurrence |
| **Nonconformity** | Non-fulfillment of a requirement |
| **Root Cause** | The underlying reason why a problem occurred |
| **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)** | A systematic process for identifying the root causes of problems |
| **Effectiveness Check** | Verification that the implemented actions have achieved the intended results |
| **5 Whys** | An iterative interrogative technique used to explore the cause-and-effect relationships |
| **Fishbone Diagram** | A visual tool for categorizing potential causes of a problem (also known as Ishikawa diagram) |

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Quality Manager
- Has overall responsibility for the CAPA system
- Reviews the adequacy of all CAPAs
- Approves CAPA closure
- Reports CAPA metrics during Management Review
- Ensures resources for CAPA activities are available

### 4.2 CAPA Coordinator
- Manages the CAPA database/system
- Assigns CAPA numbers
- Tracks CAPA status and due dates
- Sends reminders for overdue actions
- Generates CAPA metrics and reports
- Maintains CAPA records

### 4.3 CAPA Owner (Assignee)
- Leads investigations and root cause analysis
- Develops action plans
- Implements corrective/preventive actions
- Coordinates with affected departments
- Documents all CAPA activities
- Performs effectiveness checks
- Requests CAPA closure upon completion

### 4.4 Department Managers
- Provide resources and support for CAPA activities
- Participate in investigations within their area of responsibility
- Implement actions within their department
- Verify the implementation of actions

### 4.5 All Personnel
- Report nonconformities and improvement opportunities
- Participate in CAPA investigations as required
- Implement actions assigned to them
- Support CAPA effectiveness

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Corrective Action Process

Initiate corrective action for nonconformities identified from the following sources:

**Sources of Nonconformity:**
- Customer complaints (per SOP-[Number])
- Internal nonconforming product (per SOP-[Number])
- Internal audit findings (per SOP-[Number])
- Out-of-specification results from process monitoring
- Product inspection failures
- Supplier nonconformities
- Regulatory inspections or observations
- Management review action items
- Risk management outputs
- Returned or rejected products

**5.1.1 CAPA Initiation**

1. When a nonconformity is identified, the person discovering it shall:
   - Complete a CAPA Request Form (Attachment A) or enter information into the CAPA system
   - Describe the nonconformity clearly and completely
   - Attach supporting documentation (NCR, complaint, audit finding, etc.)
   - Submit to the Quality Department

2. CAPA Coordinator:
   - Receives CAPA request
   - Assigns a unique CAPA number: CAPA-[Year]-[###]
   - Registers the CAPA in the tracking system
   - Forwards to the Quality Manager for review

3. Quality Manager:
   - Reviews CAPA request for completeness and clarity
   - Determines if corrective action is required
   - Assigns priority (see Section 5.3)
   - Designates a CAPA Owner
   - Sets due dates based on priority
   - Approves initiation of the CAPA investigation

### 5.2 Preventive Action Process

Initiate preventive actions proactively to address potential problems before they occur.

**Sources of Preventive Action:**
- Trend analysis of complaints, NCRs, or other data
- Risk management activities (per SOP-[Number])
- Process capability studies
- Near-miss events
- Lessons learned from other organizations or equipment
- Changes in regulations or standards
- Proactive process improvement
- Management review outputs
- Employee suggestions

**5.2.1 Preventive Action Initiation**

The process is similar to Corrective Action (Section 5.1.1), except:
- Describe the potential nonconformity and its possible consequences
- Include data or justification supporting the need for preventive action
- Priorities may be assigned based on the risk of occurrence

### 5.3 CAPA Prioritization

All CAPAs are prioritized based on the following factors:
- Severity of impact (safety, regulatory, customer impact)
- Frequency or likelihood of occurrence
- Detectability before reaching the customer

**Priority Levels:**

| Priority | Criteria | Completion Deadline |
|----------|----------|-------------------------|
| **Critical** | Safety issues, regulatory requirements, major customer impact, potential Class I recall | [X] Days |
| **High** | Significant quality impact, repetitive issues, moderate customer impact, regulatory reporting | [X] Days |
| **Medium** | Moderate impact, isolated occurrence, minor customer impact | [X] Days |
| **Low** | Minor impact, isolated occurrence, no customer impact, improvement opportunity | [X] Days |

Priority is determined by the Quality Manager in consultation with the CAPA Owner and relevant affected Department Managers.

### 5.4 Investigation and Root Cause Analysis

**5.4.1 Investigation Plan**

The CAPA Owner develops an investigation plan, including:
- Scope of investigation
- Required team members (if applicable)
- Data to be collected
- Proposed analysis methods
- Timeline

**5.4.2 Data Collection**

Collect relevant data:
- Review relevant records (batch records, inspection records, training records, etc.)
- Interview relevant personnel
- Review previous similar occurrences
- Examine physical evidence (product samples, equipment, etc.)
- Review applicable procedures and work instructions
- Analyze trend data (if available)

**5.4.3 Root Cause Analysis**

Use appropriate RCA tools based on complexity:

**For simple problems:**
- 5 Whys technique
- Cause and effect analysis

**For complex problems:**
- Fishbone diagram (Ishikawa)
- Fault Tree Analysis
- Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)
- Statistical analysis

**RCA Requirements:**
- Dig beyond surface causes to find the root cause
- Distinguish between symptoms and causes
- Consider multiple contributing factors
- Ask "Why" repeatedly until the root cause is identified
- Consider human factors, procedural inadequacies, system deficiencies
- Document the analysis process and findings

**5.4.4 Root Cause Documentation**

Document in the CAPA record:
- Summary of investigation findings
- Identified root cause
- Supporting data and analysis
- RCA tools used
- Team members involved
- Investigation completion date

The Quality Manager reviews and approves the root cause determination.

### 5.5 Action Planning and Implementation

**5.5.1 Action Planning**

Based on the root cause, the CAPA Owner develops an action plan:

**Actions must be:**
- **Effective:** Address the root cause, not just symptoms
- **Achievable:** Realistic within existing resources
- **Measurable:** Include objective success criteria
- **Timely:** Include target completion dates
- **Risk-appropriate:** Commensurate with severity and likelihood

**Action plan includes:**
- Specific actions to be taken
- Responsible person for each action
- Target completion date for each action
- Required resources
- Expected results/success criteria
- How effectiveness will be measured

**5.5.2 Action Types**

Actions may include:
- Procedure revision or clarification
- Training or retraining
- Equipment repair, replacement, or modification
- Process change or improvement
- Design change (if applicable)
- Supplier Corrective Action Request (SCAR)
- Increased inspection or monitoring
- Software update or validation
- Physical facility changes
- Organizational structure changes

**5.5.3 Action Approval**

- CAPA Owner submits the action plan to the Quality Manager
- Quality Manager reviews the plan for adequacy and appropriateness
- Department Managers review actions affecting their areas
- Quality Manager approves the action plan
- Actions are assigned to responsible parties with due dates

**5.5.4 Implementation**

- Responsible parties implement assigned actions
- Implementation shall be documented (procedure revisions, training records, etc.)
- CAPA Owner tracks implementation progress
- CAPA Coordinator sends reminders for overdue actions
- Provide periodic updates for long-term CAPAs

**5.5.5 Implementation Documentation**

For each action, document the following:
- Implementation date
- Evidence of implementation (updated procedures, training records, work orders, etc.)
- Any deviations from planned actions and their justification
- Confirmation by the responsible person

### 5.6 Effectiveness Review

**5.6.1 Timing of Effectiveness Check**

Verify effectiveness after:
- Sufficient time has passed to observe results
- Minimum period: [X Days/Weeks] after implementation
- Extended period for process or trend verification: [X Months]
- Timing is based on priority level and the nature of the problem

**5.6.2 Effectiveness Verification Methods**

Methods applicable to CAPA may include:
- Review of process or product data to confirm performance improvement
- Inspection or test results showing improvement
- No recurrence within a specified period
- Customer feedback or complaint trends
- Internal audit verification
- Process capability analysis
- Statistical analysis of relevant metrics
- Re-audit of the corrective action area
- Follow-up inspection or testing

**5.6.3 Effectiveness Determination**

CAPA Owner:
- Collects effectiveness data using planned methods
- Analyzes data to determine if actions achieved intended results
- Documents findings in the CAPA record
- Recommends effectiveness status:
  - **Effective:** Actions achieved intended results, no recurrence
  - **Not Effective:** Actions did not achieve intended results, recurrence observed
  - **Additional Data Needed:** Insufficient time or data to determine effectiveness

**5.6.4 Ineffective Actions**

If actions are determined to be ineffective:
- CAPA remains open
- Re-investigate
- Develop alternative actions
- Repeat cycle until effectiveness is achieved

### 5.7 CAPA Closure

**5.7.1 Closure Criteria**

A CAPA may be closed when the following conditions are met:
- All planned actions have been implemented and verified
- Effectiveness check is complete and actions are determined effective
- All records are complete
- No recurrence of the problem during the effectiveness observation period

**5.7.2 Closure Process**

1. CAPA Owner:
   - Verifies all closure criteria are met
   - Completes final CAPA summary
   - Submits closure request to the Quality Manager

2. Quality Manager:
   - Reviews the entire CAPA record for completeness
   - Verifies evidence of effectiveness
   - Approves closure or request additional information
   - Signs and dates the CAPA closure

3. CAPA Coordinator:
   - Updates CAPA status to "Closed"
   - Archives CAPA records per retention requirements
   - Updates metrics and reports

**5.7.3 CAPA Extension**

If additional time is required:
- CAPA Owner submits extension request with justification
- Quality Manager reviews and approves/rejects extension
- Establishes new due date
- Extension is documented in the CAPA record
