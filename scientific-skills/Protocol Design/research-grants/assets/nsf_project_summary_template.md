# NSF Project Summary Template

**Important Note**: NSF requires the Project Summary to contain three labeled sections (maximum 1 page):
1. Overview
2. Intellectual Merit
3. Broader Impacts

---

## Overview

[Write a paragraph suitable for dissemination to the public, explaining:
- The research problem or challenge
- The research methods or approach
- Expected outcomes
- Significance

The content should be easy to understand and suitable for a broad audience, including non-scientists. Avoid using technical jargon.]

**Example:**
This project investigates how coastal wetlands respond to sea-level rise and increased storm intensity caused by climate change. By combining field observations, remote sensing, and computer modeling across 20 sites along the Atlantic coast, we will determine whether wetlands are migrating inland fast enough to keep pace with rising seas. The findings will inform coastal management policies and help predict the fate of critical ecosystems that protect shorelines and support fisheries. This work will train 5 graduate and 10 undergraduate students, prioritizing recruitment from underrepresented groups through partnerships with Minority Serving Institutions.

---

## Intellectual Merit

[Answer the following question: What is the potential for the proposed activity to advance knowledge?

Include:
- Why the research is scientifically significant
- What knowledge gaps it fills
- What will be learned
- The novelty of the research methods
- How it advances the field]

**Example:**
This study addresses a critical gap in understanding coastal wetland resilience in the context of accelerated climate change. Current wetland migration models fail to account for biological constraints on vegetation colonization and the feedback between sediment dynamics and plant growth. We will develop the first integrated model coupling hydrological, ecological, and geomorphological processes across multiple spatial scales. Our novel approach combines high-resolution LiDAR elevation data with experimental manipulations of sediment and salinity to parameterize vegetation response functions. Expected outcomes include quantitative predictions of wetland migration rates under different sea-level rise scenarios, identification of geomorphic features that facilitate or hinder migration, and new theories regarding ecosystem tipping points. This work will transform our ability to predict and manage coastal ecosystem responses to climate change.

## Broader Impacts

[Answer the following question: What is the potential for the proposed activity to benefit society?

You must address at least one of the five NSF broader impact areas through specific, measurable activities:
1. Advance discovery and understanding while promoting teaching, training, and learning
2. Broaden participation of underrepresented groups
3. Enhance infrastructure for research and education
4. Broaden dissemination to enhance scientific and technological understanding
5. Benefit society

Please provide specific activity details, timelines, and evaluation plans.]

**Example:**
This project will generate significant broader impacts through three integrated activities:

**1. Education and Training**: We will train 5 PhD students and 10 undergraduate students in interdisciplinary coastal science, focusing on field methods, remote sensing, and quantitative modeling. Undergraduates will participate through summer research internships (10 weeks, $5,000 stipend) mentored by graduate students. We will recruit 50% of undergraduates from groups underrepresented in STEM through partnerships with 4 Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs). Students will present results at the annual Biogeography Research Conference and co-author peer-reviewed publications.

**2. Stakeholder Engagement and Policy Impact**: We will collaborate with 5 state-level coastal management agencies and The Nature Conservancy to translate research findings into management tools. Annual workshops will bring together 30 coastal managers, conservation practitioners, and researchers to co-develop a decision-support framework. Results will inform state sea-level rise adaptation plans, wetland restoration prioritization, and land acquisition strategies involving 500,000 acres of coastal habitat.

**3. Public Science Communication**: We will create a publicly accessible web-based visualization tool showing predicted wetland changes across the Atlantic coast under various climate scenarios. The tool will be promoted via social media, state agency websites, and science museums, with an expected reach of 50,000 users. We will also develop bilingual (English/Spanish) educational materials for K-12 teachers and pilot them in 10 schools primarily serving underrepresented students.

Impact assessment will be conducted through pre- and post-surveys of participating students, tracking research participants into STEM careers, documenting policy adoption by management agencies, and analyzing usage metrics of the public engagement platform.

## Formatting Requirements

- **Page Limit**: Maximum 1 page
- **Margins**: 1 inch on all sides
- **Font**: 11 point or larger (Times New Roman, Arial, Palatino, Computer Modern)
- **Section Headings**: Must use these three labels exactly:
  - Overview
  - Intellectual Merit
  - Broader Impacts
- **Public Accessibility**: The Overview section must be suitable for a general audience

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Do not** omit any of the three statutory section headings
❌ **Do not** make broader impacts vague (e.g., "will train students")
❌ **Do not** use technical jargon in the Overview
❌ **Do not** exceed 1 page
❌ **Do not** forget to mention preliminary data or team qualifications
❌ **Do not** treat broader impacts as secondary (they are equally important)

✅ **Do** make all three sections substantial
✅ **Do** specify concrete broader impact activities
✅ **Do** write the Overview for a lay audience
✅ **Do** convey enthusiasm and significance
✅ **Do** proofread carefully (this is the first thing reviewers see)
