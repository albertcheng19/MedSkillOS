# Journal Formatting Requirements

Comprehensive formatting requirements and submission guidelines for major scientific journals across various disciplines.

**Last Updated**: 2024

---

## Nature Portfolio

### Nature

**Journal Type**: Premier multidisciplinary scientific journal  
**Publisher**: Nature Publishing Group  
**Impact Factor**: ~64 (varies by year)

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**: Articles approx. 3,000 words (excluding Methods, References, Figure Legends)
- **Structure**: Title, Authors, Affiliations, Abstract (≤200 words), Main Text, Methods, References, Acknowledgements, Author Contributions, Competing Interests, Figure Legends
- **Format**: Single-column for submission (final publication is double-column)
- **Font**: Any standard font (Times, Arial, Helvetica), 12pt
- **Line Spacing**: Double-spaced
- **Margins**: 2.5 cm (1 inch) on all sides
- **Page Numbers**: Required on all pages
- **Citations**: Superscript sequential numbering¹'²'³
- **References**: Nature style (abbreviated journal names)
  - Format: Author, A. A., Author, B. B. & Author, C. C. Article title. *Journal Abbrev.* **vol**, pages (year).
  - Example: Watson, J. D. & Crick, F. H. C. Molecular structure of nucleic acids. *Nature* **171**, 737–738 (1953).
- **Figures**:
  - Format: TIFF, EPS, PDF (vector preferred)
  - Resolution: 300-600 dpi for photos, 1000 dpi for line art
  - Color: RGB or CMYK
  - Size: Fits single column (89 mm) or double column (183 mm)
  - Legends: Provided separately, not embedded in the figure
- **Tables**: Editable format (Word, Excel), not as images
- **Supplementary Information**: No limit, PDF format preferred

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/nature_article.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://www.nature.com/nature/for-authors

## Science Family

### Science

**Journal Type**: Premier multidisciplinary scientific journal  
**Publisher**: American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS)

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**:
  - Research Articles: 2,500 words (main text only, excluding citations/figures)
  - Reports: Up to 2,500 words
- **Structure**: Title, Authors, Affiliations, Abstract (≤125 words), Main Text, Materials and Methods, References, Acknowledgements, Supplementary Materials
- **Format**: Single-column for submission
- **Font**: Times New Roman, 12pt
- **Line Spacing**: Double-spaced
- **Margins**: 1 inch on all sides
- **Citations**: Sequential numbering in parentheses (1, 2, 3)
- **References**: Science style (article titles are not included in main references, moved to supplementary)
  - Format: A. Author, B. Author, *Journal Abbrev.* **vol**, pages (year).
  - Example: J. D. Watson, F. H. C. Crick, *Nature* **171**, 737 (1953).
- **Figures**:
  - Format: PDF, EPS, TIFF
  - Resolution: Minimum 300 dpi
  - Color: RGB
  - Size: Max width 9 cm (single column) or 18.3 cm (double column)
  - Figures count toward page limits
- **Tables**: Included in text or as separate files
- **Supplementary Materials**: Extensive materials allowed

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/science_article.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://www.science.org/content/page/instructions-authors

## PLOS (Public Library of Science)

### PLOS ONE

**Journal Type**: Open access multidisciplinary journal  
**Publisher**: Public Library of Science

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**: No length limit
- **Structure**: Title, Authors, Affiliations, Abstract, Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusions (optional), References, Supporting Information
- **Format**: Editable files (LaTeX, Word, RTF)
- **Font**: Times, Arial, or Helvetica, 10-12pt
- **Line Spacing**: Double-spaced
- **Margins**: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
- **Page Numbers**: Required
- **Citations**: Vancouver style, numbered in square brackets [1], [2], [3]
- **References**: Vancouver/NLM format
  - Format: Author AA, Author BB, Author CC. Article title. Journal Abbrev. Year;vol(issue):pages. doi:xx.xxxx
  - Example: Watson JD, Crick FHC. Molecular structure of nucleic acids. Nature. 1953;171(4356):737-738.
- **Figures**:
  - Format: TIFF, EPS, PDF, PNG
  - Resolution: 300-600 dpi
  - Color: RGB
  - Legends: Placed after references in the main text
- **Tables**: Editable format, one per page
- **Data Availability**: Statement must be provided
- **Open Access**: All articles are open access (APC fees apply)

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/plos_one.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://journals.plos.org/plosone/s/submission-guidelines

## Cell Press

### Cell

**Journal Type**: Premier biology journal  
**Publisher**: Cell Press (Elsevier)

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**:
  - Articles: Approx. 5,000 words (excluding methods, references)
  - Short Articles: Approx. 2,500 words
- **Structure**: Summary (≤150 words), Keywords, Introduction, Results, Discussion, Experimental Procedures, Acknowledgements, Author Contributions, Declaration of Interests, References
- **Format**: Double-spaced
- **Font**: 12pt
- **Margins**: 1 inch on all sides
- **Citations**: Author-year format (Smith et al., 2023)
- **References**: Cell style
  - Format: Author, A.A., and Author, B.B. (Year). Title. *Journal* vol, pages.
  - Example: Watson, J.D., and Crick, F.H. (1953). Molecular structure of nucleic acids. *Nature* 171, 737-738.
- **Figures**:
  - Format: TIFF, EPS preferred
  - Resolution: 300 dpi for photos, 1000 dpi for line art
  - Color: RGB or CMYK
  - Multi-panel composite figures are common
- **Tables**: Editable format
- **eTOC Blurb**: 30-50 word summary required
- **Graphical Abstract**: Mandatory

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/cell_article.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://www.cell.com/cell/authors

## IEEE Transactions

### IEEE Transactions on [Various Topics]

**Journal Type**: Engineering and Computer Science journals  
**Publisher**: Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**: Varies by transaction (final format typically 8-12 pages)
- **Structure**: Abstract, Index Terms, Introduction, [Main Sections], Conclusion, Acknowledgements, References, Author Biographies
- **Format**: Double-column
- **Font**: Times New Roman, 10pt
- **Column Spacing**: 0.17 inches (4.23 mm)
- **Margins**:
  - Top: 19 mm (0.75 in)
  - Bottom: 25 mm (1 in)
  - Sides: 17 mm (0.67 in)
- **Citations**: Numbered in square brackets [1], [2], [3]
- **References**: IEEE style
  - Format: [1] A. A. Author, "Title of paper," *Journal Abbrev.*, vol. x, no. x, pp. xxx-xxx, Mon. Year.
  - Example: [1] J. D. Watson and F. H. C. Crick, "Molecular structure of nucleic acids," *Nature*, vol. 171, pp. 737-738, Apr. 1953.
- **Figures**:
  - Format: EPS, PDF (vector), TIFF (bitmap)
  - Resolution: 600-1200 dpi for line art, 300 dpi for grayscale/color
  - Color: RGB for online, CMYK for print if required
  - Position: Top or bottom of columns
- **Tables**: LaTeX table environment, placed at top/bottom
- **Equations**: Sequentially numbered

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/ieee_trans.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://journals.ieeeauthorcenter.ieee.org/

## ACM Publications

### ACM Transactions

**Journal Type**: Computer Science transactions  
**Publisher**: Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)

**Formatting Requirements**:
- **Length**: No strict limit
- **Structure**: Abstract, CCS Concepts, Keywords, ACM Reference Format, Introduction, [Main Body], Conclusion, Acknowledgements, References
- **Format**: Double-column for final, single-column for submission
- **Font**: Depends on template (typically 9-10pt)
- **Class**: Use `acmart` LaTeX document class
- **Citations**: Numbered [1] or Author-Year depending on conference/journal
- **References**: ACM style
  - Format: Author. Year. Title. Journal vol, issue (Year), pages. DOI
  - Example: James D. Watson and Francis H. C. Crick. 1953. Molecular structure of nucleic acids. Nature 171, 4356 (1953), 737-738. https://doi.org/10.1038/171737a0
- **Figures**: EPS, PDF (vector preferred), high-res bitmap
- **CCS Concepts**: Mandatory (ACM Computing Classification System)
- **Keywords**: Mandatory

**LaTeX Template**: `assets/journals/acm_article.tex`

**Author Guidelines**: https://www.acm.org/publications/authors

## Important Notes

1. **Always check official guidelines**: Journal requirements change; confirm before submission.
2. **Template Timeliness**: These templates are updated periodically but may lag behind official changes.
3. **Supplementary Material**: Most journals allow extensive supplementary materials.
4. **Preprint Policy**: Check the journal's preprint policy (most allow arXiv, bioRxiv).
5. **Open Access Options**: Many subscription journals offer paid open access options.
6. **LaTeX vs. Word**: Most journals accept both; for formula-heavy content, LaTeX is preferred.

## Obtaining Official Templates

Many journals provide official LaTeX templates:
- **Nature**: Download from the journal's website
- **IEEE**: IEEEtran class (widely available)
- **ACM**: acmart class (CTAN)
- **Elsevier**: elsarticle class (CTAN)
- **Springer**: svjour3 class (journal website)

Please visit the journal's "For Authors" or "Submit" page for the latest templates.
