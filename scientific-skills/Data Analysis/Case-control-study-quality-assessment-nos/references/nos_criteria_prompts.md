# NOS Criteria Prompts

## 1. Selection

**1.1 Is the case definition adequate?**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. For example, >1 person/record/time/process to extract information, or reference to primary record source such as x-rays or medical/hospital records.
- Negative answer marked with "-". For example, record linkage (e.g. ICD codes in database) or self-report with no reference to primary record, or no description.

**1.2 Representativeness of the cases**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. For example, all eligible cases with outcome of interest over a defined period of time, all cases in a defined catchment area, all cases in a defined hospital or clinic, group of hospitals, health maintenance organisation, or an appropriate sample of those cases (e.g. random sample).
- Negative answer marked with "-". For example, Not satisfying requirements above, or not stated.

**1.3 Selection of controls**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. Just match any of the following conditions, for example community controls (i.e. same community as cases and would be cases if had outcome), or general population (matched age, gender, and period), or both the cases and the controls originate from the same source, regional population, database. Sometimes, observed number and expected number were calculated for the affirmative answer.
- Negative answer marked with "-". For example, Hospital controls, within same community as cases (i.e. not another city) but derived from a hospitalised population, or no description.

**1.4 Selection: Definition of controls**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. For example, If cases are first occurrence of outcome, then it must explicitly state that controls have no history of this outcome. If cases have new (not necessarily first) occurrence of outcome, then controls with previous occurrences of outcome of interest should not be excluded.
- Negative answer is marked with "-". For example, no mention of history of outcome.

## 2. Comparability

**2.1 Age comparability: Was the age matched in the cases and controls?**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer.
- Negative or uncertain answer marked with "-".

**2.2 Comparability of other controlled factors: Were other controlled factors (excluding age) matched in the cases and controls?**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer.
- Negative or uncertain answer marked with "-".

*Note: Either cases and controls must be matched in the design and/or confounders must be adjusted for in the analysis. Statements of no differences between groups or that differences were not statistically significant are not sufficient for establishing comparability.*

## 3. Exposure

**3.1 Ascertainment of Exposure**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. Most centers or registries meet this criteria. For example, secure record (eg surgical records), or structured interview where blind to case/control status, or medical records, a central hospital registration system.
- Negative answer marked with "-". For example, interview not blinded to case/control status, or written self report or medical record only, or no description.

**3.2 Same method of ascertainment for cases and controls**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer.
- Negative answer marked with "-".

**3.3 Non-Response rate**
- Allocation one star (*) for the affirmative answer. The affirmative answer means that the same non-response rate for both groups were explicitly mentioned in the text.
- Negative answer marked with "-". For example, non respondents described, or rate different and no designation.
