## Template Notes

### General Principles

**Systematicity:**
- Use a consistent order (head-to-toe, outside-in)
- Do not skip relevant areas even if findings are normal
- Include pertinent negative findings

**Specificity:**
- Provide measurements (masses should have 3D sizes)
- Describe locations precisely
- Use standard terminology (RadLex)
- Quantify whenever possible

**Clarity:**
- Avoid ambiguous language
- Ensure the Impression can stand alone
- Answer clinical questions directly
- State what "is" present, not just what "is not"

**Communication:**
- Critical findings require immediate verbal notification
- Document communication details
- Provide specific recommendations
- Suggest next steps when appropriate

### Measurement Guidelines

**Lesions/Masses:**
- Three dimensions: [Length x Width x Height in cm]
- Use consistent measurement methods during follow-up

**Lymph Nodes:**
- Short-axis diameter (in cm)
- Note morphology (round vs. oval)

**Organ Size:**
- Use established normal ranges
- Adjust for age and gender

### Comparison Statements

**Improved:**
"The right upper lobe mass has decreased in size from 3.5 cm to 2.1 cm."

**Stable:**
"The 8 mm left lower lobe nodule is unchanged, stable for 2 years."

**Worsened:**
"Bilateral pleural effusions have increased, now moderate on the right."

**New Finding:**
"New 1.5 cm right adrenal nodule, not seen on prior CT."

### Differential Diagnosis Terminology

**Definite:** "Consistent with..."  
**Probable:** "Most likely represents..." or "Favors..."  
**Possible:** "Suggestive of..." or "Differential diagnosis includes..."  
**Uncertain:** "Cannot exclude..." or "Consider..."

### Recommendations

**Imaging Follow-up:**
- Specify modality, timing, and focus of evaluation
- "Recommend follow-up CT chest in 6-12 months to assess for stability"

**Further Characterization:**
- "Consider MRI for further characterization"
- "Ultrasound correlation is recommended"

**Clinical Correlation:**
- "Recommend clinical correlation with tumor markers"
- "Please correlate with patient symptoms and physical examination"

**Biopsy/Intervention:**
- "Consider biopsy for definitive diagnosis"
- "Image-guided biopsy can be performed if clinically indicated"
