## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the requirements for document control within the Quality Management System (QMS) to ensure that:

- Documents are approved for adequacy prior to issue.
- Documents are reviewed, updated as necessary, and re-approved.
- Changes and current revision status are identified.
- Relevant versions of applicable documents are available at points of use.
- Documents remain legible and readily identifiable.
- External documents are identified and controlled.
- Unintended use of obsolete documents is prevented.
- Appropriate identification is applied to obsolete documents if they are retained.

This procedure ensures compliance with the requirements of ISO 13485:2016, Clause 4.2.4.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all controlled documents within the QMS, including but not limited to:

- Quality Manual
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- Work Instructions (WIs)
- Forms and Templates
- Medical Device Files (MDF)
- Design and Development Documentation
- Risk Management Documentation
- Validation Protocols and Reports
- Specifications (Product, Process, Test Method, Material)
- Drawings and Schematics
- Labeling and Instructions for Use (IFU)
- External Documents (Standards, Regulations, Customer Specifications)

This procedure does not apply to:
- Records (controlled under SOP-[Number] Control of Records)
- Transient documents (emails, meeting notes not part of the QMS)
- Marketing and sales materials (unless they impact product quality or regulatory compliance)

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Controlled Document** | A document that is subject to review, approval, distribution control, and change management as defined in this procedure. |
| **Master Document** | The official controlled copy maintained by Document Control, from which all distributed copies originate. |
| **Document Owner** | The individual or department responsible for the content, accuracy, and maintenance of a document. |
| **Document Control Coordinator** | The person responsible for managing the document control system. |
| **Revision** | The change status of an approved and released controlled document. |
| **Obsolete Document** | A document that has been superseded by a newer version or is no longer applicable. |
| **External Document** | A document originating from outside the organization (standards, regulations, customer specifications, etc.). |
| **SUPERSEDED** | A watermark applied to obsolete documents retained for reference. |

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Document Control Coordinator
- Manages the document control system.
- Assigns document numbers.
- Maintains the master document repository.
- Ensures documents are properly approved before release.
- Distributes controlled documents.
- Maintains distribution lists.
- Recalls and destroys/archives obsolete documents.
- Maintains document control records.
- Trains personnel on document control procedures.

### 4.2 Document Owner
- Drafts and maintains documents within their area of responsibility.
- Ensures document accuracy and completeness.
- Initiates document changes when necessary.
- Participates in document review and approval.
- Identifies when documents become obsolete.
- Ensures department personnel use current versions.

### 4.3 Quality Manager
- Approves all QMS documents (SOPs, Quality Manual).
- Reviews document changes for impact on the QMS.
- Ensures the effectiveness of the document control system.
- Audits document control compliance.

### 4.4 Department Managers
- Approve department-specific Work Instructions.
- Ensure current documents are available in their areas.
- Ensure personnel are trained on document changes.
- Remove obsolete documents from points of use.

### 4.5 All Personnel
- Use only current, approved versions of documents.
- Report document issues (errors, illegibility, missing documents).
- Do not use obsolete or unapproved documents.
- Maintain documents in good condition.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Document Types and Hierarchy

QMS documentation is organized into a four-level hierarchy:

**Level 1: Quality Manual (QM)**
- Policy-level document.
- Describes the overall QMS.
- References all Level 2 procedures.
- Approved by CEO and Quality Manager.

**Level 2: Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)**
- Define "what" must be done, by "whom," and "when."
- Cross-functional processes.
- Includes all 31 controlled procedures required by ISO 13485.
- Approved by Quality Manager.

**Level 3: Work Instructions (WIs)**
- Define "how" to perform specific tasks.
- Step-by-step operational instructions.
- Department or process-specific.
- Approved by Department Manager and Quality Manager.

**Level 4: Forms and Templates**
- Standard formats for data collection.
- Support SOPs and WIs.
- Approved by Document Owner and Quality Manager.

**Other Controlled Documents:**
- Medical Device Files (MDFs)
- Design History Files (DHFs)
- Validation Documentation
- Specifications
- Drawings
- Labeling and IFUs

### 5.2 Document Numbering System

All controlled documents are assigned a unique identification number:

**Quality Manual:**
- Format: QM-[###]
- Example: QM-001

**Standard Operating Procedures:**
- Format: SOP-[ISO Clause]-[###]
- Example: SOP-4.2.4-001 (for Clause 4.2.4 Document Control)
- Example: SOP-8.5-001 (for CAPA procedure)

**Work Instructions:**
- Format: WI-[Dept Code]-[###]
- Example: WI-MFG-001 (Manufacturing Work Instruction)
- Dept Codes: MFG (Manufacturing), QC (Quality Control), ENG (Engineering), etc.

**Forms:**
- Format: FORM-[SOP/WI Number]-[Letter]
- Example: FORM-SOP-8.5-001-A (CAPA Request Form)

**Medical Device Files:**
- Format: MDF-[Product Code]-[###]
- Example: MDF-ABC-001

**Other Documents:**
- Formats vary by document type.
- Assigned by the Document Control Coordinator.

**Revision Identification:**
- Initial Release: Revision 00
- First Revision: Revision 01
- Subsequent Revisions: 02, 03, 04, etc.
- Format: [Doc Number] Rev [##]

### 5.3 Document Creation

**5.3.1 Initiating Document Creation**

1. The Document Owner identifies the need for a new document.
2. The Document Owner notifies the Document Control Coordinator.
3. The Document Control Coordinator:
   - Assigns a document number.
   - Provides document templates (if applicable).
   - Registers the document as "In Development" in the Master List.

**5.3.2 Document Formatting Requirements**

All controlled documents must include:

**Header (Every Page):**
- Document Number
- Document Title
- Revision Number
- Effective Date
- Page Number (Page X of Y)

**Document Control Sections:**
- Approval Signature Table
- Revision History Table

**Content Requirements:**
- Clear, concise language.
- Use of present tense, active voice.
- Consistent terminology.
- Numbered sections and sub-sections.
- References to related documents.
- Records generated (if applicable).

**5.3.3 Drafting the Document**

1. The Document Owner drafts the document content.
2. The Document Owner marks each page as "DRAFT."
3. Drafts may be circulated for informal review and feedback.
4. Once ready for formal review, the Document Owner submits it to the Document Control Coordinator.

### 5.4 Document Review and Approval

**5.4.1 Review Process**

1. The Document Control Coordinator:
   - Verifies the document number is correct.
   - Verifies formatting compliance.
   - Checks for completeness of required sections.
   - Distributes for review.

2. Reviewers:
   - Technical Reviewer (Subject Matter Expert).
   - Quality Reviewer (for QMS compliance).
   - Other relevant stakeholders as appropriate.

3. Review Requirements:
   - Review for technical accuracy.
   - Review for clarity and completeness.
   - Review for compliance with requirements.
   - Provide feedback to the Document Owner.

4. The Document Owner:
   - Addresses all feedback.
   - Revises the document as necessary.
   - Resubmits for approval.

**5.4.2 Approval Process**

Approval authority is determined by document type:

| Document Type | Approval Authority |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Quality Manual | CEO and Quality Manager |
| SOPs | Quality Manager |
| Work Instructions | Department Manager and Quality Manager |
| Forms | Document Owner and Quality Manager |
| Specifications | Engineering Manager and Quality Manager |
| Medical Device Files | [As per regulatory requirements] |

**Approval Steps:**

1. The Document Control Coordinator sends the document to the Approvers.
2. Approvers review and sign/date the approval block.
3. All required approvals must be obtained before the document becomes effective.
4. The Document Control Coordinator:
   - Removes the "DRAFT" watermark.
   - Adds the Effective Date (typically [X] days after approval).
   - Finalizes the format.
   - Adds to the controlled document system.
   - Updates the Master Document List.

**5.4.3 Training Requirements**

Before a document becomes effective:
- Affected personnel are identified.
- Training is conducted as required.
- Training records are maintained per SOP-[Number].

### 5.5 Document Distribution and Access

**5.5.1 Master Documents**

- The Document Control Coordinator maintains the master copies.
- Masters are stored in: [Electronic System or Physical Location].
- Masters are clearly identified as "MASTER COPY."
- The master is the source for all distributed copies.

**5.5.2 Controlled Copies**

**Electronic Distribution (Primary Method):**
- Documents are stored in the [Document Management System].
- Access is controlled by user permissions.
- Most users have read-only access.
- Only the current revision is displayed.
- Obsolete versions are automatically removed from the access path.
- Users may print for immediate use (Uncontrolled Copies).

**Physical Distribution (When Necessary):**
- Controlled copies are issued for specific locations/uses.
- Each copy is stamped "CONTROLLED COPY - [Copy Number]."
- A Distribution List is maintained, showing:
  - Copy Number
  - Document Number and Revision
  - Holder Name and Location
  - Date Issued
- Holders are responsible for keeping copies in good condition.
- When a document is revised, Document Control recalls the old copy and issues the new one.

**5.5.3 Uncontrolled Copies**

- Printed for temporary, immediate use.
- Stamped or marked "UNCONTROLLED COPY."
- The user is responsible for verifying the current revision before each use.
- Should be destroyed after use or within [X] days.

**5.5.4 Availability at Points of Use**

- Current documents are available at locations where operations are performed.
- Workstations have electronic access.
- Controlled physical copies are provided in areas without electronic access.
- Documents are protected from damage, loss, or deterioration.

### 5.6 Document Changes

**5.6.1 Initiating Changes**

Changes can be initiated by:
- Document Owner identifying a need.
- CAPA requiring a procedure change.
- Internal audit findings.
- Management Review improvements.
- Regulatory or standard updates.
- Process improvements.

**5.6.2 Change Request Process**

1. The Requestor:
   - Completes a Document Change Request (DCR) form (Attachment A).
   - Describes the requested change and the reason.
   - Submits to the Document Owner.

2. The Document Owner:
   - Reviews the change request.
   - Determines if the change is appropriate.
   - Approves or rejects the request.
   - If approved, initiates the document revision.

**5.6.3 Implementing Changes**

1. The Document Owner:
   - Requests the current master from Document Control.
   - Creates a revision incorporating the changes.
   - Increments the revision number.
   - Updates the Revision History table.
   - Identifies changes in the document (side change bars, highlighting, or summary).
   - Marks as "DRAFT REVISION."

2. Change Review and Approval:
   - Approval authority is the same as the original document.
   - Unless the original approvers designate a different authority.
   - Reviewers have access to the previous version for comparison.

3. Approval:
   - The new version is approved per Section 5.4.2.
   - The previous version becomes obsolete on the effective date of the new version.

**5.6.4 Identification of Changes**

Changes are identified by:
- Revision History table (describing the nature of the change).
- Change bars or highlighting within the document (optional but recommended).
- Summary of changes page for major revisions (optional).

**5.6.5 Emergency Changes**

For urgent changes affecting safety or regulatory compliance:
- An expedited review and approval process is used.
- Temporary methods (e.g., hand-written changes with approval signatures) may be used.
- Formal document revision must be completed as soon as practicable.
- If the change is due to a non-conformity, it is documented per the CAPA process.

### 5.7 Control of Obsolete Documents

**5.7.1 When a Document Becomes Obsolete**

A document becomes obsolete when:
- A new version is approved and effective.
- The document is no longer applicable to operations.
- A product is discontinued.
- A process is changed.

**5.7.2 Withdrawal from Points of Use**

1. The Document Control Coordinator:
   - Marks the superseded version as obsolete on the effective date of the new version.
   - Removes from the electronic document system or adds an "OBSOLETE" note to restrict access.
   - Recalls physical controlled copies from distribution locations.
   - Updates the Distribution List.

2. Department Managers:
   - Remove obsolete copies from work areas.
   - Return to Document Control or destroy.
   - Ensure personnel are aware of the new version.

**5.7.3 Retention of Obsolete Documents**

Obsolete documents may be retained for:
- Reference purposes.
- Product investigations.
- Regulatory or legal requirements.
- Historical records.

**If retained:**
- They must be clearly marked "OBSOLETE - FOR REFERENCE ONLY" or "SUPERSEDED."
- Stored separately from current documents.
- Access is restricted and controlled.
- Retained according to applicable retention requirements.

**Retention Location:**
- Electronic archive with "OBSOLETE" watermark.
- Separate physical archive area.

**5.7.4 Prevention of Misuse**

To prevent unintended use:
- Physical copies are stamped with a red "OBSOLETE" stamp.
- Electronic copies have an "OBSOLETE" watermark.
- Removed from active work areas.
- Kept in a separate archive repository.
- Personnel are trained on the document control system and how to verify current versions.

### 5.8 Control of External Documents

**5.8.1 Types of External Documents**

External documents include:
- ISO Standards.
- IEC Standards.
- FDA Regulations and Guidance Documents.
- EU Regulations (MDR, IVDR).
- Other regulatory requirements.
- Customer specifications.
- Supplier certificates.
- Calibration certificates.
- Reference materials.

**5.8.2 Identification and Control**

1. The Document Owner or Requestor:
   - Identifies the required external document.
   - Provides a copy to the Document Control Coordinator.

2. The Document Control Coordinator:
   - Assigns an external document number: EXT-[Category]-[###].
   - Records in the External Document Register, including:
     - Document Title and Number.
     - Source/Publisher.
     - Date/Version.
     - Location within the organization.
     - Person responsible for monitoring updates.
   - Files in the external document repository.

**5.8.3 Currency Review**

- Document Owners are responsible for monitoring updates to external documents.
- Frequency: [Annually or upon notification of update].
- Check publisher websites for updates.
- Subscribe to update notifications where available.
- When an update is identified:
  - Obtain the new version.
  - Provide to Document Control.
  - Review impact on QMS documentation.
  - Update QMS documentation as needed (via CAPA if necessary).
  - Obsolete the previous version per Section 5.7.

**5.8.4 Customer-Supplied Documents**

- Customer specifications and drawings are controlled as external documents.
- Reviewed for clarity and completeness upon receipt.
- Discrepancies are resolved with the customer.
- Controlled under this procedure to ensure current versions are used.
