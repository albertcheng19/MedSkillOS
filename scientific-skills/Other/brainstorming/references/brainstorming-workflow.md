# Brainstorming Workflow (Detailed Version)

## Objectives and Scope

- **Objective:** Transform user-proposed ideas into clear, discussable directions—clarifying first, then diverging, and finally converging into actionable next steps.
- **Applicable to:** Scenarios requiring deep understanding and exploration, such as new features, component design, strategy selection, creative ideation, and behavioral adjustments.
- **Not applicable to:** Minor changes or clearly defined tasks (which can be executed directly without prolonged discussion).

## General Principles

- Ask only one question at a time; split into multiple rounds if necessary.
- Prioritize multiple-choice questions for quick alignment, then use open-ended questions to supplement details.
- Diverge first, then converge; avoid finalizing a solution at the very beginning.
- Confirm "correct understanding" after every output.

## Detailed Process

### 1) Clarifying Ideas and Objectives

- Restate the user's idea to confirm mutual understanding.
- Fill in objectives, audience, scenarios, boundaries, and constraints (time, resources, technical limitations, etc.).
- Identify key contradictions or uncertainties and record them as questions for subsequent exploration.

**Example questions:**
- What problem do you ultimately hope this will solve?
- Who is the most important audience or user?
- Which constraints are non-negotiable?

### 2) Diverging Ideas and Segmenting Dimensions

- Deconstruct the problem by dimensions: user needs, value proposition, implementation path, risk points, and metrics.
- Propose 2-3 possibilities for each dimension, maintaining as much differentiation as possible.
- Provide brief pros and cons for each possibility without drawing a conclusion.

**Example dimensions:**
- **Experience:** Intuitiveness vs. Customizability
- **Cost:** Short-term fast output vs. Long-term maintainability
- **Technology:** Stable solution vs. Exploratory solution

### 3) Convergence and Recommendation

- Narrow down feasible solutions to 1 recommendation + 1 alternative.
- Clarify the reasons for the recommendation and why other options were discarded.
- Output a "Next Steps Action List" to ensure progress continues.

**Suggested output format:**
- **Recommended Solution:** One-sentence definition + 2-3 reasons
- **Alternative Solution:** One-sentence definition + 1-2 trade-offs
- **Next Steps:** 3-5 actionable items

### 4) Alignment and Confirmation

- Ask if the understanding is correct or if adjustments are needed after each output.
- If there are conflicts or unclear information, return to Step 1 for further clarification.

## Interaction Rhythm Suggestions

- **Short before long:** Use short sentences and lists for alignment first, then dive into details.
- **Small steps in multiple rounds:** Avoid overly long outputs that make it difficult for the user to confirm.
- **Lightweight visualization:** Use short, structured lists when necessary; avoid stacking long paragraphs.

## Delivery Standards

- User ideas are clearly restated.
- Objectives and constraints are clear.
- At least 2 directions with trade-off explanations are provided.
- Actionable next steps are provided.
